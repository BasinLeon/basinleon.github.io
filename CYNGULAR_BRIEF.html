Architecture

CIRA Logic Applied to Revenue

Your product operates on a clear principle: detect threats, investigate context, respond with precision. A revenue engine should work the same way. The discipline should not change just because the domain shifts from security to sales.

CIRA (Cyber)

Detect — Threat signals across multi-cloud environments.

Investigate — Automated triage, correlation, and context.

Respond — Targeted neutralization with precision.

Revenue Engine (GTM)

Detect — Buying signals: CISO job changes, cloud migrations, compliance shifts.

Investigate — ICP scoring, contact verification, engagement history.

Respond — Personalized outreach referencing the exact trigger. Under 5 minutes.

Proposal

What I Would Build for Cyngular

  1. Signal Detection

    Automated monitoring for the events that indicate a company is actively evaluating cloud security: CISO appointments, breach disclosures, cloud migration announcements, regulatory changes. These are the demand signals your competitors are missing or responding to too late.

  2. Governed Enrichment Pipeline

    Every signal passes through a logic gate before anyone acts on it. ICP fit, company size, tech stack verification, prior engagement check. Nothing fires without passing governance. No spam, no brand risk, full audit trail. The same standard you would expect from any system touching Cyngular's name.

  3. Precision Response

    Qualified signals trigger outreach that references the specific event. A CISO who just announced a multi-cloud migration receives a message about CIRA's investigation capabilities in that exact environment. Response time drops from days to minutes.

  4. Pipeline Visibility

    Real-time view of every qualified opportunity: where it came from, what signal triggered it, where it sits in the funnel, and what action is needed. No Friday spreadsheet updates. The board sees pipeline health the same way your SOC sees threat posture — live.

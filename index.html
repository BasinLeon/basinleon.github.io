The Story: Why I Started Coding at 40

After 15 years managing GTM teams, I realized something: most revenue leaders operate in abstraction. We architect systems on whiteboards, design processes in Notion, then hand it off to engineering and hope it works.

So I started coding. Not to become a software engineer—but to eliminate the translation layer between strategy and execution. When you can architect a revenue system and immediately prototype it, test it, and deploy it, you move at a different speed.

That's why I built BASIN::NEXUS. That's why I've written 83,000+ lines of code. That's why I orchestrate 5 LLMs in production. Not because I'm a developer—because I'm a Revenue Architect who codes.

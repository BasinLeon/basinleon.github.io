01 / Market entry

Project::
Sentinel

Turn noisy security demand into qualified commercial motion.

Measured outcome 160% year-over-year U.S. pipeline growth
Problem

Enterprise security demand was buried under noisy prospecting and inconsistent qualification.

Constraint

Lean team, strict ICP, and no headcount expansion to absorb the mess manually.

Decision

Build two routing lanes with explicit qualification rules, clear ownership, and stronger signal preservation.

Result

Pipeline velocity improved while commercial workflow automation generated $424K in annual operating savings.

What I would improve

Instrument sales-stage conversion earlier, so closed-lost language and stage movement sharpen the qualification model sooner.

02 / Scale architecture

Project::
Delight

Separate the motions without splitting the operating truth.

Operating outcome 2 lanes one shared governance layer
Problem

One-size-fits-all routing was slowing enterprise and SMB motion at the same time.

Constraint

Existing stack only, no additional hiring budget, and no appetite for heavy operating drag.

Decision

Split enterprise and SMB into parallel lanes while preserving shared definitions, ownership, and governance.

Result

Inbound and outbound coverage scaled more cleanly without adding headcount to compensate for confused routing.

What I would improve

Introduce intent scoring one quarter earlier and review threshold drift before routing behavior becomes habit.

03 / AI signal operations

Project::
Aurora

Make AI earn operator trust before it earns more autonomy.

Measured outcome 92% intent accuracy · roughly 4× throughput
Problem

Technical account briefs took too long to produce, and signal quality varied across researchers.

Constraint

Fortune 500 security accounts, a high accuracy threshold, and no room for generic AI output.

Decision

Build confidence-gated brief generation with explicit review logic and a shared signal-to-prediction model.

Result

Research throughput improved roughly fourfold while intent accuracy reached 92% on the evaluated workflow.

What I would improve

Feed closed-lost analysis into the model earlier and keep deeper operating specifications private unless permission and context support review.

04 / The pattern

What repeats across the work.

The industries and tools change. The underlying operating failure is remarkably consistent.

Interpret before automating.

Speed only compounds value after the team agrees on what the signal means.

Make ownership explicit.

A routing model is incomplete until one person owns the next action and the feedback loop.

Keep the work legible.

The system should be explainable enough to inspect, teach, challenge, and improve.

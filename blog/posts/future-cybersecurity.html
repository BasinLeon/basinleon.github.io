Future-Proofing Cybersecurity: The GTM Engineer's Approach to AI, Web3, and 2035

Cybersecurity is changing faster than most GTM teams can adapt. The threat landscape of 2035 will look nothing like today—and your messaging needs to anticipate that shift.

The Three Waves

Wave 1 (Now - 2027): AI-augmented attacks vs. AI-powered defense. Zero Trust becomes mandatory, not optional.

Wave 2 (2027 - 2031): Post-quantum cryptography migration. Anyone selling today needs to be positioning for this.

Wave 3 (2031 - 2035): Decentralized identity and Web3 security infrastructure. The concepts sound abstract now; they'll be table stakes.

The GTM Engineer's Role

You're not just selling features. You're helping buyers navigate uncertainty. The companies that win will be the ones who sound like they understand where security is going—not just where it's been.

That requires a different kind of GTM: less pitch, more education. Less features, more architecture. Less urgency, more trust.

Harnessing AI for Healthcare: Applications, Risks, and Ethical Insights

Healthcare is one of the highest-stakes domains for AI. The potential is enormous: faster diagnoses, personalized treatment, drug discovery acceleration. But so are the risks.

Applications that work: Radiology image analysis, clinical documentation, drug interaction checking, administrative workflow automation. These augment human judgment without replacing it.

Risks to manage: Bias in training data, hallucination in clinical recommendations, privacy breaches, over-reliance on algorithmic outputs. The stakes are literally life and death.

Ethical framework: AI in healthcare should always be supervised by clinicians, auditable in its reasoning, and transparent in its limitations. "The model says" is never sufficient—human judgment remains essential.

The goal isn't AI healthcare. It's AI-augmented healthcare that makes human providers more effective.

### 2. Technical Depth: Clay + Enrichment
**Question:** "Walk me through your Clay experience."

**Answer:**
"I use Clay as the orchestration layer for lead enrichment. Here's my typical workflow:

1. **Ingest:** I pull leads from multiple sources (LinkedIn, Apollo, USASpending.gov for federal contracts) into Clay tables.
2. **Enrich:** I use Clay's native enrichments (Clearbit, Apollo, etc.) plus custom Python scrapers I've built. For example, I wrote a scraper that pulls funding data from Crunchbase and news signals from Google News API.
3. **Score:** I send the enriched data to a Python scoring algorithm (hosted on AWS Lambda) that uses a simple ML model to predict 'likelihood to buy' based on signals like recent funding, tech stack, and job changes.
4. **Route:** High-scoring leads get pushed to Salesforce and Slack. Low-scoring leads go to a nurture campaign in HubSpot.

The key is that Clay is just the orchestration layer. The real magic happens in the custom Python logic I've built around it."

### 3. AI-First GTM Automation
**Question:** "How do you think about AI in GTM?"

**Answer:**
"I think about AI in GTM in three layers:

**Layer 1: Enrichment** - AI helps you gather better data. For example, I use GPT-4 to analyze company websites and extract 'pain points' that sales can use in outreach.

**Layer 2: Scoring** - AI helps you prioritize. I've built a lead scoring model that uses historical win/loss data to predict which leads are most likely to convert. It's not perfect, but it's 70% accurate, which is way better than random.

**Layer 3: Execution** - AI helps you act. For example, I've built an AI agent that drafts personalized outreach emails based on the enriched data. The SDR just reviews and sends.

The mistake most companies make is they try to jump straight to Layer 3 (AI writes all the emails!) without nailing Layer 1 and 2. You need clean data and smart prioritization before you can automate execution."

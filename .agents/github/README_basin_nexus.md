# Basin::Nexus
**Signal-Driven GTM Architecture for Modern Revenue Systems**

## Overview
Most GTM systems waste time upstream.

They allow weak-fit accounts, shallow intent, and noisy data to enter the pipeline too early. The result is predictable: wasted outreach, low conversion, fragmented follow-up, and rep time spent on the wrong opportunities.

**Basin::Nexus** is a signal-driven GTM architecture designed to improve decision quality before execution begins.

It answers a practical question:

**Which signals are worth acting on, and what should happen next?**

## Who This Is For
Basin::Nexus is built for:
- technical founders
- RevOps and GTM leaders
- operators designing smarter revenue systems
- teams that want better decision logic before adding more automation

## What This Is
- **A GTM decision layer:** A framework for evaluating fit, intent, urgency, and trust before action is taken.
- **An architecture portfolio:** A working model for how modern revenue systems can score, route, and prioritize opportunities.
- **A bridge between signal and execution:** The layer between raw market inputs and downstream CRM or sales workflows.

## What This Is Not
- **Not a generic SaaS tool:** This is not another automation layer added on top of a broken process.
- **Not a public code dump:** The full logic, prompts, and internal workflows remain private.
- **Not a scraping project:** Data collection without decision logic is just automated noise.

## Core Architecture
Basin::Nexus operates in three stages:

1. **Signal Layer**  
   Ingests inputs such as hiring changes, funding events, executive transitions, ecosystem movement, and internal account context.

2. **Decision Layer**  
   Scores each opportunity across four variables:
   - **Fit**
   - **Intent**
   - **Urgency**
   - **Trust**

3. **Action Layer**  
   Routes the next best motion:
   - nurture
   - human review
   - targeted outreach
   - executive engagement
   - no action

## The Signal Model
Not all signals deserve pipeline attention.

Basin::Nexus evaluates opportunities through a four-variable model before authorizing action:

- **Fit:** Does the account meet structural requirements?
- **Intent:** Is there evidence of active interest or relevant market movement?
- **Urgency:** Is there a time-sensitive reason to act now?
- **Trust:** Has enough credibility or context been established to justify action?

### Gating Logic
Fit is a hard gate. If structural fit is missing, the process stops.

Trust acts as a penalty factor. High intent with low trust is not enough to justify automated action.

This prevents the common failure mode of modern GTM: mistaking visibility for readiness.

## The Action Model
Once a signal clears the scoring gates, Basin::Nexus recommends the next motion:

- **Tier 1: Nurture**  
  High fit, low urgency. Continue monitoring and collecting stronger signals.

- **Tier 2: Architected Outreach**  
  High fit, moderate intent. Route to a human with context-rich briefings and suggested messaging.

- **Tier 3: Executive Motion**  
  High fit, high urgency, strong trust. Escalate to founder, AE, or strategic operator.

## Why It Matters

More tools do not fix weak logic. More automation does not fix poor signal selection.

Basin::Nexus is built on a simple belief:

**The edge in modern GTM comes from making better decisions before execution scales.**

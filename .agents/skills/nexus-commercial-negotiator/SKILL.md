# Nexus Commercial Negotiator

The **Nexus Commercial Negotiator** is designed to protect the 'Sovereign Status' of the operator. It ensures that pricing is anchored on structural value, not hourly labor.

## Core Principles

1. **The Professional Anchor**: Always lead with a fixed-fee pilot (e.g., $2,000/mo) to build core infrastructure. This signals that you are an Architect, not a 'task-taker'.
2. **The Negotiated Floor**: Pre-calculate a 'floor' (e.g., $1,800/mo) that maintains profitability while meeting client budget triggers.
3. **The 'O-Factor' Trigger**: Tie renegotiation to a specific structural milestone (e.g., 1,000 Active Users or 90% Retention), rather than a calendar date. This forces alignment on growth.
4. **Outcome-Led Attachments**: Reference 'Mirrors' and 'Yield Briefs' as the deliverables, not hours worked.

## Workflow

### 1. Structure the Anchor
- Define a 90-day pilot timeframe.
- Pair the base fee with a clear 'Resource Commitment' (e.g., 5 high-focus hours per week).

### 2. The Follow-up Protocol
- Send a 'Strategic Yield Brief' immediately after meetings to maintain momentum.
- Explicitly mention next steps: Approval -> Formal Agreement -> Weekend Initialization.

## When to Use
- When a client asks "what do you charge?"
- When moving from a 'consultant' role to a 'fractional GTM owner'.
- When structuring 90-day pilots for startups.

# Nexus Economic Intelligence Protocol

Use this skill to research, analyze, and act on the deeper economic forces that drive enterprise decision-making.

## High-Value Economic Indicators

1. **Capital Velocity**: Mapping venture funding rounds and PE acquisitions to predict infrastructure needs. (e.g., "$1.2B in venture funding gaps mapped").
2. **Talent Concentration**: Identifying regional shifts in hiring (e.g., "The Tel Aviv/NY Cybersecurity Hub move").
3. **Budgetary Triggers**: Locating events that unlock enterprise spending (Fiscal year shifts, leadership changes, regulatory compliance deadlines).
4. **Efficiency Ratios**: Benchmarking the cost of signal acquisition ($0.25/signal) against the potential contract value ($1M+ LTV).

## The Signal Refinery (Economic Version)

* **Level 1 (Raw Signal)**: Hiring post for a "Director of GTM."
* **Level 2 (Inferred Pain)**: The company is scaling but likely lacks the underlying infrastructure.
* **Level 3 (Economic Action)**: Deliver a "Revenue Architecture" audit showing $424K in potential manual-labor savings.

## Strategic Economic Yield

* Focus on **Regional Hubs** rather than individual targets.
* Build **Yield Forecasts** that show the client the "Cost of Delay" vs the "ROI of Infrastructure."
* Use **Zero-Storage Security** as an economic differentiator in high-compliance markets.

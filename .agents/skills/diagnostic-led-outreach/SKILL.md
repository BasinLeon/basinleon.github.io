# Diagnostic-Led Outreach

## Overview

Stop being a "Vendor" and start being an "Architect." This skill uses the "Diagnostic Wedge" to bypass HR gatekeepers and enter the room of Executive Leadership. Instead of pitching a solution (e.g., training), you pitch a **Diagnostic Brief** that identifies the technical or mechanical friction in the client's business model.

## When to Use

- When a client asks for a "Proposal" or "Course List."
- When a company is experiencing a **THE_PIVOT** (e.g., Linear to Digital, Manual to Agentic).
- When you need to justify higher fees by solving structural problems rather than delivering tasks.

## Core Principles

### 1. The "Brief" Over the "Brochure"

Never send a menu of services. Send a roadmap of outcomes. Use a "Diagnostic Brief" to show you understand the underlying business friction.

| Use (Vendor) | Use (Architect) |
| :--- | :--- |
| "Here is our pricing" | "Here is the revenue roadmap" |
| "Our training modules" | "The execution framework" |
| "Proposal" | "Diagnostic Brief" |
| "Sales skills" | "New business revenue motion" |

### 2. Commercial Language vs. Architecture Jargon

Maintain the strategy but use language that resonates with Sales and Business Leadership.

| Internal Jargon (Avoid) | Commercial Language (Use) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Signal Layer | How the team identifies opportunities |
| Discovery Architecture | Running discovery in a new revenue motion |
| Signal-to-Close Loop | The new business execution model |
| GTM Engineering | Revenue Architecture |

### 3. Identify the "Business Friction"

Every request has a "Why Now." Find the underlying friction and name it.
- **Example (Media)**: "The challenge isn't prospecting; it's the shift from legacy Linear relationships to high-velocity Digital hunting."

## The "Strategic Brief" Template

Use this structure to replace standard proposals:

1.  **Context**: "Based on the shift to [New Model]..."
2.  **Observed Challenge**: Identify the specific friction (e.g., "Confidence gap in new business dev").
3.  **Execution Gaps**: Where the current motion fails (e.g., "Discovery is stalling").
4.  **Recommended Intervention**: "A structured sprint to implement the [New] motion."
5.  **Expected Outcomes**: Measurable lift in velocity or conversion.
6.  **Timeline & Investment**: Clear, outcome-based phases.

## Example: The "NBC Move"

> **Context**: NBC HR asking for AE training for a digital pivot.
> 
> **Architect Reply**: 
> "Instead of a standard training proposal, I'm putting together a 'Digital Revenue Diagnostic' for your leadership team. It maps out exactly where the prospecting confidence is breaking down in the shift from Linear to Digital. It gives your leadership a look at the revenue leakage before we even talk about training schedules."

## Rationalization Table

| Excuse | Reality |
| :--- | :--- |
| "They asked for a proposal" | They asked for a *solution*. A proposal is just a format. |
| "I don't want to sound too smart" | Sounding *useful* is better than sounding *smart*. Use commercial language. |
| "A catalog is easier for them" | A catalog makes you a commodity. A diagnosis makes you a partner. |

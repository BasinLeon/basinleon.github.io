# Obvio Public-Safe Publisher

## Purpose
Convert private operational proof into public-safe positioning. The public story should communicate capability without exposing customers, territories, stakeholders, deal values, internal workflows, or attackable details.

## Redaction Pass
Before drafting public content, remove or abstract:

- Customer, prospect, partner, city, agency, or stakeholder names
- Deal values, pipeline totals, renewal amounts, quotas, and forecast numbers
- Contact details, titles tied to named accounts, or internal reporting chains
- Outreach scripts, channel tactics, prompt chains, and play-by-play sequences
- CRM object design, import rules, data schemas, DNC lists, and tooling specifics
- Any sensitive timing window or active market-entry strategy

## Safe Public Frames
Use these instead:

- "Built GTM operating systems for early-stage market entry."
- "Designed research-to-outreach workflows across regulated public-sector markets."
- "Created executive reporting loops that turned field activity into decision-ready signal."
- "Built private second-brain infrastructure for pipeline memory, account planning, and weekly operating cadence."
- "Developed repeatable playbooks for territory research, stakeholder mapping, and high-signal outreach."

## Public Output Tiers
Choose the lowest-risk tier that satisfies the request:

- **Green**: role, capability, sanitized system design, general outcomes.
- **Amber**: anonymized metrics or generalized examples. Use only when needed.
- **Red**: named accounts, cities, stakeholders, values, active tactics, internal architecture. Keep private.

## Website Guidance
For `basinleon.github.io`, publish only durable identity and capability:

- Current role and company
- Broad operating thesis
- Sanitized systems built
- Public writing or case-study style patterns without active details

Never publish the private GTM Command Center itself.

## Final Check
Ask internally:

- Could a competitor infer active targets?
- Could a social engineer use this against the user or employer?
- Could this violate employer trust?
- Does this expose internal operating leverage?

If any answer is yes, keep it private or abstract further.

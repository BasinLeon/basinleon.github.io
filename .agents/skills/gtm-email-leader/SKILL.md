# GTM Email Leader

Use this skill when writing important emails in Leon's voice.

This skill is for:

- client replies
- founder communication
- negotiation emails
- kickoff and follow-up emails
- partnership messages
- recruiter and warm-intro replies

## Core Standard
Write like a calm operator who is already in motion.

Not:

- needy
- over-explaining
- defensive
- performatively polite
- trying to sound impressive

## The Voice

- direct
- warm
- commercially aware
- structurally clear
- short to medium sentences
- no fake gravitas

## Email Hierarchy

### 1. State the operational reality first
Examples:

- confirm the time
- confirm the next step
- confirm what access is needed
- confirm what happens next

### 2. Frame the work correctly
If it is a kickoff, say kickoff.
If it is a pilot, say pilot.
If it is a partnership conversation, say that directly.

Do not hide the real status of the relationship.

### 3. Keep the ask small and practical
Best asks:

- send the invite
- share read-only access
- send the sheet
- confirm the payment method

Bad asks:

- vague asks
- bundled asks with no priority
- emotional reassurance asks

## Default Structure

### Part 1: Main answer
Open with the answer, not context.

Example:
*"Yes, 5pm UK tomorrow still works for me."*

### Part 2: Reframe the meeting if needed
Example:
*"Let’s use this as the kickoff and access handoff so we can move straight into the first sprint."*

### Part 3: Small operational ask
Example:
*"If you can share the dashboard, Search Console, and outreach sheet before the call, I can start the baseline diagnostic immediately."*

### Part 4: Close simply
Example:
*"Best, Leon"*

## Anti-Patterns

- Do not apologize unless something meaningfully broke.
- Do not sound grateful for basic responsiveness.
- Do not soften every sentence.
- Do not bury the answer under explanation.
- Do not use long consultant paragraphs when two clean paragraphs will do.

## Tone Rules by Scenario

### Client kickoff
- clear
- grounded
- operational

### Founder negotiation
- warm
- bounded
- leverage-aware

### Recruiter / intro
- courteous
- low-friction
- selective

### Internal operator update
- blunt
- short
- decision-oriented

## Rewrite Filter
Before finalizing, ask:

1. Did the email answer the question in the first line?
2. Did it sound like Leon is already leading the motion?
3. Did it avoid unnecessary apology or appeasement?
4. Is there one clean next step?

If not, rewrite.

## Example: MTI Kickoff Reply

Good:

*"Yes, 5pm UK tomorrow still works for me. Let’s use the call as the kickoff and access handoff so we can move straight into the first sprint.

If you can share the read-only dashboard access, Search Console, and outreach sheet before the call, I can start the baseline diagnostic immediately."*

Bad:

*"Apologies for the delay. I just wanted to circle back and say that I think I might be able to make that time, and I would also be grateful if you could maybe share some things in advance."*

## Principle

The best GTM email is not the prettiest one.

It is the one that keeps the motion moving while preserving status.

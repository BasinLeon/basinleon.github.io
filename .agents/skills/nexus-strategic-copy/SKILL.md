# Nexus Strategic Copy Protocol

Use this skill when drafting emails, pitch decks, landing page micro-copy, or strategic reports. This protocol enforces the **Leon Move**—air-tight, senior, and direct.

## Voice & Tone

1. **The Senior Operator**: Speak as someone who manages infrastructure, not just tasks. Use "Deploying," "Ingesting," and "Compiling" instead of "Writing" or "Doing."
2. **Outcome Over Activity**: Never talk about the process unless it's to prove the yield. (e.g., "20 targets in 68 seconds" vs "I found some companies").
3. **The Revenue Architect**: Use "Logic Leaks," "Signal Refinery," and "Deterministic ROI."
4. **Human-First**: Avoid the "I hope this email finds you well" and "Let's dive in" clichés. Start with the signal or the result.

## Frameworks

* **S2Y (Signal-to-Yield)**: Frames the value in terms of ROI per signal.
* **The Ladder of Status**: Always position the user as a peer to the founder/CRO, not a vendor.
* **Micro-Commitments**: End copy with low-friction, high-logic next steps.

## Forbidden Patterns (AI Detection)

* No "delve," "unlock," "unleash," or "comprehensive."
* No triple-point summaries (The Rule of Three is okay, but avoid "In conclusion, X, Y, and Z").
* No promotional enthusiasm. Use "Direct/Senior" instead.

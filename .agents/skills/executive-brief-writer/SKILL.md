# Executive Brief Writer

## Overview

Your power is in 1-page thinking. This skill generates concise documents that move decision-makers. It avoids the "pitch deck" fluff and focuses on commercial reality.

## Structure of the Brief

A standard Executive Brief follows this exact structure:

1.  **Context**: The "Why Now" (e.g., industry shift, hiring trigger).
2.  **Observed Challenge**: The structural friction (identified via `strategic-reframe`).
3.  **Diagnostic Lens**: How we will look at the problem (e.g., The 4 Focus Areas).
4.  **Proposed Intervention**: 3-Phase roadmap (not a service list).
5.  **Expected Outcomes**: Commercial impact (not activity metrics).
6.  **Next Step**: The micro-commitment (e.g., "Schedule a call to finalize architecture").

## Core Principles

- **No Marketing Fluff**: No "About Us" or "Testimonials" in the brief.
- **Vertical Hierarchy**: One idea per section. Max 1-2 sentences per point.
- **Authority Voice**: Direct statements. Avoid "We believe..." or "I think...".

## Types of Briefs

- **Diagnostic Brief**: Used to wedge into a new deal (see NBC example).
- **Founder Note**: High-level alignment on a partnership play.
- **GTM Assessment**: A post-sync summary of findings.

## Example: The "Leon" Move
- **Standard**: A 20-page proposal with case studies.
- **Leon**: A 1-page "Revenue Architecture Memo" that names the leakage points.

## Integration
This skill works alongside:
- `strategic-reframe`: To find the right "Observed Challenge."
- `artifact-generator`: To produce the final markdown/document.

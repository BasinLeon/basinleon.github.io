# Strategic Reframe

## Overview

The "Signature Move." This skill takes a surface-level request (training, tools, headcount) and reframes it as a structural or architectural challenge. It stops the conversation from being about "services" and starts the conversation about "revenue infrastructure."

## When to Use

- When a client asks for "better prospecting" or "more meetings."
- When a founder asks for "help with sales."
- When the user asks "How do I respond to this request for training?"

## Core Principles

### 1. Tactical vs. Structural

| Client Request (Tactical) | Reframe (Structural) |
| :--- | :--- |
| "We need better prospecting." | "We need to audit the Signal Refinery architecture." |
| "Our reps need to close more." | "The Discovery Architecture doesn't match the new buyer motion." |
| "We are hiring 10 SDRs." | "You are scaling the 'Manual Grind' instead of the systems logic." |
| "We need a new CRM." | "The GTM data spine is fragmented." |

### 2. The Reframe Protocol

1.  **Acknowledge the Pain**: Identify the surface symptom.
2.  **Locate the System Failure**: Pivot to the underlying mechanism.
3.  **The "You're Looking at This Wrong" Move**: Explain why the tactical solution won't fix the structural problem.
4.  **Propose the Diagnostic**: Offer to map the architecture before touching the tools.

## Examples

### Example: Sales Training
- **Input**: "We need to train our AEs to close more digital deals."
- **Reframe**: "This isn't a training issue; it's a revenue transition problem. The AEs are using linear sales mechanics in a high-velocity digital environment. We need to redesign the discovery motion before we train them on it."

### Example: Hiring
- **Input**: "I'm hiring 5 SDRs to scale our outbound."
- **Reframe**: "Adding headcount solves volume, but it doesn't solve yield. You're scaling the 'Manual Grind'. We should look at a Signal Refinery approach to decouple your output from your payroll."

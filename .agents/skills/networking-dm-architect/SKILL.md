# Networking DM Architect

## When to Use

Use this skill when drafting short-form messages to recruiters, hiring managers, or potential partners. This is for LinkedIn, Twitter (X), or cold email.

## Triggers

- "Write a DM to [Person]"
- "Networking note for [Role]"
- "Fix my message to the recruiter"
- "Short introduction for [Company]"

## The Process

### 1. No "AI Niceties"

Humans don't say "I hope this finds you well" or "Of course! I'd be happy to help." They start with the point.

- **Bad**: "I hope you're having a wonderful week! I saw your recent post and thought it was a testament to your leadership."
- **Good**: "I saw your note about the Ketch role. Most teams miss the activation gap in a new channel; happy to share how we fixed that at Fudo."

### 2. Shorter Sentences (Human Rhythm)

Don't use three clauses when one will do. Break long sentences into two.

- **Bad**: "Given my extensive experience in the Cybersecurity sector and my background in building GTM systems, I believe I can be of service to your organization."
- **Good**: "I built the US channel program at Fudo. We scaled it from zero to $10M in pipeline using a 5-day ramp cycle."

### 3. One Specific Ask

Don't offer "to grab coffee and pick your brain." Offer specific value or ask one direct question.

- **Good**: "Are you hitting any friction with the current reseller onboarding? I've got a short doc on the 5-day ramp logic if it's helpful."

### 4. Remove the "AI vocabulary"

- **No**: additionally, align with, crucial, delve, fostering, garner, highlight, landscape, pivotal, showcase, tapestry, testament, underscore, vibrant.
- **Yes**: use, build, help, show, fix, see, know, do.

## Example: Before vs After

### Before (AI-Sounding / Vague)

> Hi Leon! I hope you're having a great day. I am reaching out because I saw your experience at Fudo Security and I believe it serves as a testament to your expertise in the field. I would love to delve deeper into your background and see how we can align our goals. Would you like to schedule a call?

### After (Networking DM Architect / Simple English)

> Hi Leon,
> I saw you built the US channel at Fudo. Most security firms struggle to translate EMEA models to the US market—usually because of the 'Activation Gap'. If you're currently hitting that friction, I've got a short logic doc on the 5-day ramp we used.
>
> Happy to send it over if that's a priority right now.

## Output Checklist

1. Is the intro sentence under 10 words?
2. Is the total message under 75 words?
3. Did you remove "I hope this finds you well"?
4. Is there exactly one clear call to action (CTA)?

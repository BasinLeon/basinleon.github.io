# Resume Architect

## When to Use

Use this skill when the task involves writing, updating, or optimizing a resume, CV, or professional bullet points. Specifically for roles in GTM, Partnerships, Sales, and Operations.

## Triggers

- "Write my resume"
- "Fix my bullet points"
- "Optimize for [Job Description]"
- "Make my experience sound more senior"

## The Process

### 1. Identify Outcomes (Signal-to-Yield)

Before writing, identify the specific economic impact of each role.

- **Bad**: "Responsible for managing a team of sales reps."
- **Good**: "Scaled a 12-person outbound team that generated $1.4M in new pipeline."

### 2. Remove "AI Slop"

Enforce "Simple English" by stripping common LLM writing patterns:

- **No inflated symbolism**: Avoid "serves as a testament to," "marks a pivotal moment," or "evolving landscape."
- **No rule-of-three**: Don't force list items into groups of three (e.g., "seamless, intuitive, and powerful").
- **No present participle (-ing) fluff**: Replace "highlighting the importance of..." with direct result statements.
- **No vague attributions**: Avoid "Industry experts believe" or "Known for." Use specific data instead.

### 3. Use "Revenue Architect" Voice

- **Direct**: Start bullet points with strong active verbs (Scaled, Built, Recovered, Engineered).
- **Structural**: Focus on the *mechanics* of how you got the result, not just the result itself.
- **High-Status**: Use calm, direct language. Avoid "thrilled," "passionate," or "expert."

### 4. Optimize for the "Logic Gap"

When tailoring for a job description, identify the "Logic Gap"—the specific structural problem the company is trying to solve (e.g., "They have a great product but don't know how to activate partners"). Address that gap directly in the summary or bullet points.

## Example: Before vs After

### Before (AI-Sounding / Vague)

> - Managed the Americas channel program, serving as a testament to my commitment to strategic growth.
> - Collaborated with cross-functional teams to ensure a seamless, intuitive, and powerful partner experience.
> - Highlighted the importance of data quality in the evolving technological landscape.

### After (Resume Architect / Simple English)

> - Built the Americas Channel program from zero, adapting the EMEA model for the US market.
> - Recovered a stalled $30,000 enterprise deal by engineering a parallel-path co-sell motion.
> - Integrated AI-driven intent signals into HubSpot, increasing partner response rates by 125%.

## Output Checklist

1. Is every bullet point an outcome, not a responsibility?
2. Are all "AI vocabulary" words (delve, bolster, tapestry, etc.) removed?
3. Is the sentence structure varied (not all the same length)?
4. Does it fit on a single page?

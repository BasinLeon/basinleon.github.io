# Strategic Question Generator

## Overview

Great operators ask better questions than everyone else. This skill generates "Structural Questions" that break the frame and force the client to confront reality. These are the tools used to validate a `strategic-reframe`.

## Core Principles

### 1. The Frame-Breaker
Avoid "How" and "What" questions that lead to tactical answers. Use "Mechanical" questions.

| Tactical Question | Strategic (Kill-Shot) Question |
| :--- | :--- |
| "How is prospecting going?" | "Where in the pipeline do digital opportunities actually stall?" |
| "What are your Q2 targets?" | "What is the delta between your linear yield and your digital ceiling?" |
| "Do you have a CRM?" | "What reason usually shows up in the CRM for a lost deal, and how often do you believe it?" |
| "How many SDRs do you need?" | "At what headcount does your current execution model start to see diminishing returns on yield?" |

### 2. The "Diagnostic Three"
Always prepare at least three kill-shot questions for any discovery call:
1.  **The CRM Question**: Exposes the gap between rep perception and reality.
2.  **The Stalling Question**: Locates the specific mechanical friction point.
3.  **The Learning Question**: Exposes the complexity of the transition (e.g., "What's hardest for a new hire to learn?").

## When to Use

- Preparing for a discovery call (e.g., Target, NBC, Sentry).
- Replying to an email where the client seems stuck in a tactical frame.
- Conducting a "Revenue Audit."

## Integration
Pairs with `diagnostic-led-outreach` and `strategic-reframe`.

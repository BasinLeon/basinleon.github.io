# Revenue Architect Proposal Audit

## When to Use
Use this skill when reviewing a proposal, pitch deck, or scope of work that sounds too "vendor-y," tactical, or service-heavy. Your goal is to elevate it into a strategic advisory and infrastructure build.

## The Audit Framework

### 1. The Language Shift (Vocabulary Audit)
Replace tactical "service" words with structural "architecture" words.

| Avoid (Tactical) | Prefer (Structural) |
| :--- | :--- |
| Training / Program | Infrastructure / Deployment |
| Skills / Confidence | Capabilities / Mechanics |
| Group / Team | Engine / System / Architecture |
| Workshop | Audit / Build / Optimization |
| Help / Support | Alignment / Strategic Control |

### 2. The ROI Pivot
Tactical proposals focus on "better skills." Structural proposals focus on **"Yield & Leakage."**
- **The Hook**: "You have a proven team, but your current revenue architecture is leaking $X in digital transition."
- **The Value**: "We don't just teach reps to sell; we build the system that makes selling inevitable."

### 3. The Roadmap Refactoring
Standard proposals use a linear "Step 1, Step 2" approach. Refactor them into a "Refinery" or "Execution Map."

- **Phase 1: Diagnostic** (Identify the Revenue Gap).
- **Phase 2: Build/Deploy** (Construct the Digital Revenue Infrastructure).
- **Phase 3: Optimize** (Ensure the Mechanics Mesh with the Team).

### 4. Injecting the "Agentic" Hook
Always check: Does the proposal mention how AI/Automation (the "Signal Refinery") reduces the human burden?
- If it's pure human training, it's a 2010 proposal. 
- A "Revenue Architect" proposal includes **Automation as a Force Multiplier.**

## High-Status Rules
1. **Never "Help"**: You don't help people; you **align** systems or **engineer** growth.
2. **Architecture over Methodology**: Don't sell a "Methodology" (Sandler, etc.); sell an **Architecture** that incorporates the best parts of all of them.
3. **The Hero Constraint**: Frame the proposal so it makes the internal champion (e.g., Kevin) look like a structural innovator, not just someone buying a trainer.

## Example: Before vs. After

### Before (Jassi Style)
"We will train your 21 AEs in Tech Sales Mastery to boost their confidence in digital deals."

### After (Leon Style)
"We are deploying a Digital Revenue Infrastructure for your 21 AEs to bridge the $50k transition gap. This isn't training; it's a structural realignment of your discovery mechanics to match non-linear buyer behavior."

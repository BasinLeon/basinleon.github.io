# Executive Keynote Mastery

## Use when
Use this skill when the user needs to prepare a presentation, a significant interview answer, or a public talk. This moves from "Sharing Information" to "Controlling the Room."

## The STARK Narrative Framework
Every artifact generated by this skill must follow the STARK model:
1.  **Signal**: Identify the overwhelming trend or data point that cannot be ignored.
2.  **Tension**: Expose the gap between the current "Manual Grind" and the potential "Automated Future."
3.  **Architecture**: Present the solution not as a "task," but as a permanent structural fix.
4.  **Result**: Quantify the yield (Time, Revenue, or Sanity).
5.  **Kinetic**: Define the immediate "next move" to maintain momentum.

## Presentation Principles
- **Minto Pyramid**: Lead with the answer/recommendation first, then provide the supporting logic.
- **Visual Pacing**: One core idea per slide/moment. No bullet-point soup.
- **The "Billion-Dollar Pause"**: Instructions to the speaker on where to stop to let a high-status point land.

## High-Status Reframing
- **Bad**: "I'm here to talk about my experience with CRM."
- **Mastery**: "Most companies are operating with a $200k logic-leak in their CRM. I'm going to show you how to plug it."

## Output Formats
- **Marp Slides**: Professional Markdown-based slides.
- **Speaker Script**: A "Narrative Track" with vocal cues (e.g., "[Lower tone here]", "[Pause for 3 seconds]").
- **Executive Brief**: A one-page "Leave-behind" that summarizes the architecture.

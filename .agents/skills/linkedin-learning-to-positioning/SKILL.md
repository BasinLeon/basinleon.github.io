# LinkedIn Learning To Positioning

## Overview
Convert LinkedIn Learning history, declared skills, and publications into a cleaner operator story. This skill is for translating scattered learning behavior into credible positioning, proof, and targeted self-development narratives.

## When to Use
- You have `Learning.csv`, `Skills.csv`, or `Publications.csv`
- You want to tighten resume, profile, portfolio, or interview positioning
- You need to identify skill clusters instead of listing everything flat
- You want to separate legacy skill residue from current strategic capability

Do not use this when the goal is course recommendations from the internet. This skill works from exported first-party history.

## Inputs
- `Learning.csv`
- `Skills.csv`
- `Publications.csv`
- Optional: `Endorsement_Given_Info.csv`
- Optional: `Certifications.csv`, `Recommendations_Received.csv`, `Endorsement_Received_Info.csv`

## Process
1. Group the learning history into capability clusters:
   - AI
   - GTM / sales
   - leadership
   - productivity
   - cybersecurity
   - adjacent operator skills
2. Split declared skills into:
   - current differentiators
   - supporting skills
   - legacy or low-signal residue
3. Pull publications into a proof layer:
   - long-form authorship
   - subject matter themes
   - narrative differentiators
4. Review endorsements given for reciprocity and affinity:
   - who you publicly vouched for
   - which skill clusters you most often recognized in others
   - where your outbound endorsement behavior overlaps with your target network
5. Build a positioning matrix:
   - `What the record says you studied`
   - `What the profile says you can do`
   - `What proof exists in writing or outputs`
6. Produce reusable language for:
   - About section
   - resume summary
   - interview framing
   - operator bio

## Output Shape
- Capability clusters with representative evidence
- Current-vs-legacy skill map
- Positioning statements grounded in export evidence
- Gaps between target role narrative and current signal surface

## Heuristics
- Weight recent learning behavior more than old generic skills
- Weight publications and shipped artifacts above passive course consumption
- Treat endorsements given as weak-but-useful signal about who you recognize, support, and stay close to
- Treat breadth as supporting evidence, not the main story
- Compress noisy skill lists into a small number of strategic themes

## Common Mistakes
- Listing every skill equally
- Confusing course watching with true proof
- Letting older low-signal skills dominate the current narrative
- Ignoring publications that show differentiated voice or domain depth

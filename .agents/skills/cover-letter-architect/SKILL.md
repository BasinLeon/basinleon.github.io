# Cover Letter Architect

## When to Use

Use this skill when drafting cover letters, application notes, or recruiter DMs. This is for high-stakes GTM, Partnerships, and Leadership roles where "Standard Applicant" language is a disadvantage.

## Triggers

- "Write a cover letter"
- "Draft an application note for [Company]"
- "Apply to this job/role"
- "Fix my cover letter"

## The Process

### 1. Identify the "Revenue Leak" (Diagnostic Analysis)

Instead of starting with "Who I am," start with "What I see." Research the company's current challenge (e.g., they're moving from PLG to Enterprise) and lead with why that specific transition is hard.

- **Bad**: "I'm a seasoned GTM leader with 10 years of experience."
- **Good**: "Most teams moving from a self-serve model to enterprise partnerships hit a wall when their enablement playbook doesn't translate to a 6-month sales cycle."

### 2. Remove "Application Slop"

Enforce "Simple English" by stripping common "application-speak":

- **No "I am thrilled/excited/passionate"**: These are weak, low-status markers. Use "I saw," "I noticed," or "I've been thinking about" instead.
- **No "Highly motivated/detail-oriented"**: Show, don't tell.
- **No "I am a perfect fit"**: This is an opinion, not a fact. State what you've *done* that solves their specific problem.

### 3. Use the "Revenue Architect" Voice

- **Direct**: Communicate like a peer (Diagnostic Advisor), not a subordinate.
- **Concise**: No more than three short paragraphs.
- **Mechanism-Focused**: Explain the *how*. "I built the system that did X" is better than "I have experience in X."

### 4. Close with a Low-Friction Step

Don't ask for a "30-minute call to discuss my background." Ask for a specific reaction to a diagnostic insight.

- **Good**: "If you're already seeing friction in your partner activation, I'm happy to share the specific logic we used to ramp our EMEA partners in 5 days instead of 90."

## Example: Before vs After

### Before (AI-Sounding / Vague)

> Dear Hiring Manager,
> I am absolutely thrilled to apply for the Partnerships Lead role at Ketch. As a highly motivated professional with a proven track record of success, I am confident that my unique background in strategic alliances and GTM architecture makes me a perfect fit for your evolving technological landscape. I have a commitment to innovation and look forward to discussing how I can add value to your team.

### After (Cover Letter Architect / Simple English)

> Hi [Name],
> I saw Ketch is building out its partner channel. Usually, the biggest revenue leak in this phase isn't the partner recruitment—it's the 'Activation Gap' where partners sign on but don't know how to co-sell your specific privacy-first logic to enterprise clients.
>
> At Fudo, I solved this by building an automated 'Partner-in-a-Box' framework that reduced ramp time for our Americas resellers from 90 days to 5 days. We recovered $30k in stalled deals in the first week by simply fixing the enablement path.
>
> Happy to share that framework if you're currently hitting friction in the onboarding phase.

## Output Checklist

1. Does it skip the "I'm thrilled" intro?
2. Does it lead with a specific diagnostic insight about the company or role?
3. Is it under 200 words?
4. Are all "AI vocabulary" and "Application slop" words removed?

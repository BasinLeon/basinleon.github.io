TalSmart: Visitor Journey & Structure Audit

PREPARED BY LEON BASIN // MARCH 23, 2026 // STRUCTURE ONLY, NOT TEXT

Jassi, this audit is only about page structure, not the words on the page. I went through each page the way a cold visitor would: landing from Google, LinkedIn, or a referral link. What follows are the exact moments where someone gets confused, loses the thread, or leaves. Each one has a specific fix.

Page 1 of 5 — Homepage
Homepage Visitor Journey
talsmart-homepage.netlify.app
Section 1 Hero — Generic headline
Section 2 Before / After grid
Section 3 Cinematic claim
Section 4 Revenue Execution System (4 cards)
Section 5 Case studies (search page)
Section 6 Coaches
Section 7 Testimonials
Section 8 Blog, then footer (dead end)
Walk-Away
No primary conversion action in the hero
Visitor lands and sees "Structured Sales Growth," which describes the company but doesn't hook anyone. Two CTAs compete ("Start Diagnostic" and "See It In Action") and neither tells the visitor what actually happens when they click.
One dominant CTA above the fold. The diagnostic is the entry point, so make it the only hero action. Move "See It In Action" down to Section 3 (the cinematic section).
Walk-Away
Page ends on blog articles, no closing conversion
After 8 sections of scrolling, the visitor hits 3 blog teasers and a footer. There is no final call to action. The page just stops.
End the page with a full-width "Start Your Diagnostic" block (same energy as the hero). Move blog to the nav or a sidebar.
Confusion
Revenue Execution System cards all link to different pages, no guidance on where to start
The 4-card layout (Diagnostic, Mastery, Coaching, Search) gives equal visual weight to each option. A VP of Sales looking at this doesn't know whether to click the diagnostic or the coaching page first. There is no routing logic.
Add a line above the grid: "Not sure where to start? Take the 3-minute Revenue-Gap Diagnostic." Make the Diagnostic card larger, bordered, and labeled "Start Here."
Confusion
Leadership Search case studies appear before the coaches section
A visitor scrolling down sees executive placement stories before they know who the coaches are. This creates a "wait, is this a recruiting firm?" question mid-page.
Swap the order: Coaches first, then testimonials, then case studies. Show who you are, then show what they did.
Page 2 of 5 — Revenue-Gap Diagnostic
Diagnostic Page Visitor Journey
talsmart-revenue-gap-diagnostic.netlify.app
Section 1 Hero — "Losing deals at one stage"
Section 2 4 pain blocks (wall of text)
Section 3 20-min assessment explainer
Section 4 5-stage breakdown
Section 5 Report contents (duplicate list)
Section 6 Pricing tiers
Section 7 RES cross-sell, then footer
Walk-Away
20-minute commitment is the only entry point, too much friction for first touch
A visitor who clicks "Start Your Free Diagnostic" expects 2-3 minutes. When they find out it is 20 questions and 20 minutes, most leave. The page does a good job educating, but there is no fast-track option.
Add a "3-Minute Quick Scan" (5 questions) as the low-friction entry. The full 20-minute version becomes the "Deep Diagnostic," positioned as the upgrade after they have already started.
Confusion
"What's inside your diagnostic report" lists the same items twice
The same 5 report components appear as a checklist and then again as expanded cards right below it. The visitor reads the same thing twice. Looks like a copy-paste error.
Keep one format. The expanded cards are better. Cut the checklist.
Confusion
Pricing tiers look identical, Free vs Team difference is invisible
The Individual (free) and Team (paid) tiers have nearly the same feature list. The real differences are buried in small text. A VP scanning fast can not tell why they would pay for the team version.
Make the Team tier the visual default (larger, highlighted border). Use a side-by-side comparison table so the VP can scan the difference in 5 seconds.
Walk-Away
PDF report delivered via email = dead end
Visitor finishes the diagnostic and gets a PDF in their inbox. They open it once, skim it, close it. There is no reason for them to come back to TalSmart after that.
Supplement the PDF with a live dashboard at a unique URL. The visitor can share it with their VP. Each gap on the dashboard links directly to the Mastery module that fixes it, so the upsell path is built into the deliverable.
Page 3 of 5 — Tech Sales Mastery Program
Tech Sales Mastery Visitor Journey
talsmart-tech-sales-mastery.netlify.app
Section 1 Hero — "Stop losing deals"
Section 2 Why MEDDIC isn't enough
Section 3 5-stage curriculum (long scroll)
Section 4 Cinematic claim
Section 5 Tri-fold framework
Section 6 Before/After table (4+4)
Section 7 Testimonials
Section 8 Pricing, case studies, blog
Walk-Away
5-stage curriculum is one long vertical scroll with no navigation
All 5 stages (Prospecting through Negotiation) stack vertically with 6 sub-items each. That is 30+ line items in a single scroll. A VP scanning this page will lose patience around Stage 3. There is no way to jump to the stage they care about.
Convert to tabs or an accordion. Clicking "Discovery" expands only that stage. Default to Stage 1 open. Show 2-3 outcomes per tab, not the full syllabus.
Confusion
$20 entry price and $400 full program compete in the same visual space
The hero says "Start with Prospecting, $20." Stages 2-5 each say "Get All 5 Stages, $400." Both prices appear in the curriculum section before pricing has been framed. The visitor sees "$400" before they understand what they are getting.
Pull pricing out of the curriculum cards entirely. Keep it only in the dedicated pricing section. Curriculum cards use "Explore Stage" links. The pricing section anchors the $20 trial as the obvious starting point.
Confusion
No connection between "I took the diagnostic" and "which stage should I buy"
A visitor coming from the Diagnostic page already has their gap data. But the Mastery page does not acknowledge that. There is no bridge saying "Your diagnostic found Discovery as your gap, here is the module that fixes it."
Add a banner at the top: "Already taken the Revenue-Gap Diagnostic? Your report shows which stages to start with." This ties the two pages together and makes the funnel feel like one system.
Walk-Away
Case study links point nowhere
Three case study teasers ("Read Case Study") all link back to the current page. The visitor clicks, nothing happens. It looks broken.
Build the actual case study pages, or remove the links and embed the proof inline (stats, pull quotes, no click required).

Section 5 4-step process
Section 6 Case studies + results

Section 8 Testimonials, CTA, footer
Walk-Away
No pricing, no packaging, visitor cannot self-qualify
The Mastery page shows $20 / $400. The Coaching page shows nothing. A VP reading this has no idea whether coaching costs $5K or $50K. The only action is "Book a Discovery Call," which asks for a calendar commitment before the visitor knows if coaching is even in their budget.
Add a "Starting at" range or tiered packaging ("Single Rep Coaching, $X/mo" vs "Full Team, Custom"). Let the visitor self-qualify before asking them to book time.
Confusion
Engagement detail section (6 blocks) reads like a contract, not a selling page
"Competency Gap Audit," "Weekly Deal Reviews," "Bi-Weekly Rep Coaching," "Adoption Tracking," "Manager Programme," "Executive Reviews." These 6 blocks describe internal mechanics. The visitor does not need to know the cadence. They need to know what changes.
Restructure around 3 outcomes: (1) "Your reps execute consistently" (2) "Your managers actually coach" (3) "Your pipeline becomes predictable." Put the process detail behind a "How it works" accordion.
Confusion
Coach bios appear after case studies, credibility sequence is backwards
The visitor reads about "Top 5% Globally" and "34% shorter deal cycles" but does not know who did it yet. By the time they scroll to the coach bios, the connection is gone.
Put coach bios before case studies. Show who they are first, then what they did, then what clients said about it.

Confusion
6 role types all look the same, no filtering
CRO, VP of Sales, Head of GTM, VP RevOps, Director Enterprise Sales, Regional VP all appear as identical cards. A visitor looking for a CRO scrolls past 5 irrelevant roles. Feels like a generic recruiter listing, not a curated search.
Feature the top 2 roles (CRO, VP of Sales) with full descriptions. Condense the other 4 into a "We also place:" row. Or add a tab/filter.
Walk-Away
This page is completely disconnected from the other 4 pages
Zero references to the Diagnostic, Mastery, or Coaching pages. A visitor from Google searching "executive search" has no idea TalSmart does anything else. A visitor who came from the homepage does not see how Search connects back to the rest of the system.
Add a strip above the footer: "The Revenue Execution System" showing all 4 components with "You are here: Leadership Search" highlighted. Every sub-page should have this indicator.
Confusion
Search Brief form buried below a long FAQ section
The main conversion action ("Submit Your Search Brief") sits below 8+ FAQ answers. By the time someone scrolls that far, they have either already left or forgotten what they came to do.
Put the brief form above the FAQ. The flow should be: Process, Guarantee, Results, "Ready? Submit Your Brief," then FAQ for anyone who still has questions.

System-Wide Structural Issues (All 5 Pages)

1. No persistent "Revenue Execution System" navigation bar
Every sub-page feels like its own standalone website. A visitor on the Coaching page has no idea it is step 3 of a 4-part system. Add a sticky sub-nav or breadcrumb: Diagnostic / Mastery / Coaching / Search.
2. No cross-page "Next Step" bridging
Each page ends at its own footer. There is nothing like "Done with the Diagnostic? Your next step is Tech Sales Mastery." The pages do not move the visitor forward. They just end.
3. Footer nav links point to the current page or placeholder anchors
Several footer links (About, Coaches, Framework, Advisory) redirect to the same page the visitor is already on, or go to "#". This looks broken. Every link needs to either work or be removed before launch.
4. No urgency or scarcity structures on any page
No seat limits, no cohort deadlines, no "3 coaching slots left this quarter." Every CTA says the same thing: take your time. Enterprise buyers move faster when there is a constraint.
5. Mobile scroll depth is extreme
The Mastery and Coaching pages are 15+ screen-heights on mobile. Without accordions, tabs, or collapsible sections, a mobile visitor will leave before they ever reach the CTA. The structure needs to compress on smaller screens.

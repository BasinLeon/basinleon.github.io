## The framework model

## 1) Signal

Definition: a market event that creates immediate candidate movement or institutional pressure.

Signal examples:
- Institutional signal: Makers Academy cohort graduation date in the next 7 to 14 days
- Corporate signal: right-sizing event at Monzo or Revolut
- Ecosystem signal: funding pause, hiring freeze, or role consolidation across a target employer cluster

Signal quality test:
- Is it time-bound?
- Is there identifiable talent attached?
- Can outreach be deployed within 24 to 48 hours?

If any answer is no, do not strike yet.

## 2) Intercept

Definition: a signal-aware outreach strike that feels human, specific, and useful.

Intercept message structure:
- Context: acknowledge the signal
- Relevance: why this matters now for them
- Offer: fast readiness path
- Ask: short next step

Example:
"Saw the Monzo update. We are running a fast-track placement readiness lane for impacted talent and career teams. Worth a 15-minute review this week?"

## 3) Validation

Definition: convert inbound interest into verified readiness, not generic lead volume.

Validation workflow:
- 15-minute readiness assessment
- score candidate signal on communication, role fit, and interview readiness baseline
- classify into readiness tiers

Tier model:
- Tier A: Strike-ready now
- Tier B: 1 to 2 week calibration needed
- Tier C: not ready for partner handoff yet

Output to partner:
- Verified Strike-Ready shortlist
- clear role alignment notes
- risk flag if candidate is not yet handoff-safe

## 4) Yield

Definition: the revenue and velocity captured from validated signal flow.

Yield channels:
- placement commission flow (15 percent)
- monthly operating retainer stability (500)
- institutional proof expansion into adjacent cohorts

North-star operating equation:
Signal quality x Intercept speed x Validation accuracy = Placement yield

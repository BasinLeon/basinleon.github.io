Why this exists

Training improves readiness. It does not guarantee flow.

The risk after enablement is simple: the team is sharper, but qualified digital opportunities still enter too slowly, too broadly, or too late. This pilot fixes the handoff zone between readiness and live revenue motion.

What stays intact
NBC keeps the current sales system This starts as an external lead-generation and refinement layer, not a CRM replacement.
NBC keeps seller ownership Qualified opportunities still route to the right AE, market owner, or sales leader.
NBC proves it with a baseline comparison Leadership can compare quality, response speed, and downstream progression before expanding scope.
The Gap After Training

The issue is not effort. It is routing, timing, and fit.

NBC is asking sellers to move from linear inventory conversations to digital solution selling. The broader TalSmart engagement is designed to improve readiness, confidence, and execution behavior. The activation layer solves the next problem: making sure higher-fit digital opportunities reach the right seller while intent is still live.

What breaks today

Interest arrives through inbound and outbound channels, but quality and urgency are inconsistent. Stronger reps still spend time sorting weak opportunities.

What this pilot changes

NBC gets an additional, externally managed layer that identifies target buyers, filters them against agreed priorities, and hands over better opportunities faster.

Non-Disruption Rules

Start outside the system. Earn the right to go deeper later.

This matches the direction in the Jassi syncs: keep the first engagement easy to buy, easy to measure, and easy to stop if the signal is weak.

No immediate internal integration The pilot can run alongside current workflows and feed approved leads into the existing process.

No giant transformation ask on day one Leadership sees one market, one pilot, one benchmark, then decides whether the model deserves a wider deployment.

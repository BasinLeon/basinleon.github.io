# Revenue Architecture | Executive Cheatsheet
## The Talk Track for High-Status GTM

Use this cheatsheet to bridge the gap between internal system technicals and executive outcomes. Move the conversation from "training" to "infrastructure."

---

### Phase 1: Signal Ingestion
**[Signal Refinery]**
- **The internal logic:** Automated processing of 44k+ nodes (hiring, funding, tech-stack shifts).
- **Executive Pitch:** "We stop guessing who to call. The Refinery identifies real buying intent across the market and scores it against our ICP before a rep even sees it."

---

### Phase 2: Friction Identification
**[Logic Leak]**
- **The internal logic:** Points where manual friction causes deal drop-off.
- **Executive Pitch:** "A Logic Leak is where your revenue system is broken at the process level. It’s not a personnel issue; it’s a design flaw where high-value intent is hitting a manual bottleneck and dying."

**[Structural Diagnostic]**
- **The internal logic:** The audit of the revenue engine.
- **Executive Pitch:** "Instead of reviewing sales calls, we perform a Structural Diagnostic. We look at the revenue engine as a piece of infrastructure to find where the signal is being lost."

---

### Phase 3: Execution & Velocity
**[Decision Velocity]**
- **The internal logic:** The time from 'Signal' to 'Closed Win'.
- **Executive Pitch:** "We don't just want more leads; we want Decision Velocity. By removing the Logic Leaks, we compress the time it takes for a customer to say 'Yes' once they enter the system."

**[Strike Actuator]**
- **The internal logic:** The automated or agent-led touchpoint.
- **Executive Pitch:** "The Strike Actuator is the tactical trigger. It’s the mechanism that delivers the right high-status message at the exact moment of peak buyer urgency."

---

### The "Airtight" Narrative Transition:
- **If they say "Training":** You say "Infrastructure."
- **If they say "Coaching":** You say "System Calibration."
- **If they say "Hiring more":** You say "Extracting more yield from the existing architecture."

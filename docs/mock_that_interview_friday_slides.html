The real opportunity

Mock That Interview already has real signal: live product, active pricing, SEO traction, and early B2B interest. The next bottleneck is not just getting more leads. It is turning demand into repeat usage, paid behavior, and a B2B offer institutions will actually buy.

This is not primarily a lead-generation problem. It is a conversion, retention, packaging, and GTM-operating problem.

What is already working

  • Roughly 200 users prove there is real market interest.
  • Organic search is already creating discoverability.
  • The product has enough surface area to support both individual and institutional value.
  • There is already inbound curiosity from B2B buyers.

What needs to tighten

  • Onboarding and first-session activation.
  • What happens after the first interview session.
  • How users come back and become paid users.
  • How an institution clearly understands what it is buying.

Market focus

  • Bootcamps and career accelerators should be the first B2B wedge.
  • Universities should stay in discovery and pilot mode for now.
  • The fastest path is to prove one repeatable, retained, institutional use case.

What I want to pressure-test in the demo

  • First-time user flow.
  • Repeat-usage triggers.
  • Admin or cohort value for organizations.
  • Where pricing and packaging feel strong or weak.

## 1. THE CORE FRAMEWORK: "The 3D Business Model"
Jassi teaches a manual scoring system to identify high-propensity buyers based on **CHANGE**.

### The Scoring Logic (0-10 Scale)
*   **0-4 (Status Quo):** "Perfectly fine, no need to buy." (Dead Lead).
*   **5-7 (Friction):** "Things are annoying." (Warm Lead).
*   **8-10 (Crisis/Opportunity):** "Huge shift happening." (Hot Lead).

### The "Spider Web" Visualization (Radar Chart)
Jassi visualizes this as a **Radar Chart (Spider Web)** with 8 axes:
1.  **Strategy** (Global Expansion?)
2.  **Revenue** (Losses? Growth?)
3.  **Technology** (Migration? Legacy?)
4.  **Organization** (Hiring? Layoffs?)
5.  **Product** (New Launch?)
6.  **Competition** (New Entrants?)
7.  **Political** (Leadership Change?)
8.  **Culture** (Morale?)

### The "Deep Dish" Case Study (The Whale)
*   **Strategy (8):** Going Global.
*   **Revenue (9):** Lost $200k/weekend (Crisis).
*   **Technology (9):** Moving to Proprietary (Migration).
*   **Organization (7):** Understaffed.
*   **Total Score:** **43** (The Threshold is 40).

### The "Hot Spot" Rule
*   **Score < 40:** "Keep Digging / Nurture."
*   **Score > 40:** "Strike Now." (Position as the 'Ultimate Sidekick').
*   **Key Insight:** The "Hot Spots" are the axes furthest from the center (8s and 9s). Pitch *only* to those pains.

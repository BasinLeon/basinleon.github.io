# Business Case: GTM Academics & Institutional Scaling

## The Challenge
Most EdTech and career-readiness platforms (MTI included) stall at the B2C level, fighting for $20-$50 individual subscriptions. This results in high CAC (Cost Per Acquisition) and low-LTV (Lifetime Value) as users churn immediately after landing a job.

## The Nexus "Academic Hub" Intervention
For the **MockThatInterview (MTI)** project, Basin::Nexus identified a massive logic gap: Universities and Bootcamps are the ultimate "Whales" for placement platforms. They have large buckets of budget but are often technically archaic in how they track "Placement Readiness."

### Core Strategy Deployed:
1.  **The "Placement Readiness Score" Hook:** Moving beyond simple mock interviews into a diagnostic dashboard that provides the *Institution* with a real-time signal of which students are ready for market and which are lagging.
2.  **Territory Segmentation:** 
    *   **The Saudi/UAE Bridge:** Leveraging Garrett's network in the Middle East education sector to find high-value, high-budget institutional pilots.
    *   **The Silicon Valley MBA Channel:** Using Santa Clara University as a benchmark for high-status, US-based career accelerators.
3.  **Frontline Deployment:** Utilizing Ola to manage the high-volume SDR outreach specifically to program directors at global bootcamps (Africa, India, UK).
4.  **PLG for Institutions:** Using a "Free Pilot Window" for the first 100 students to prove ROI, then transitioning into a bulk license agreement.

## The Outcome & Yield
*   **Pipeline Development:** Mapped the path from 243 organic users to 1,000+ Institutional users within 90 days.
*   **Commercial Logic:** Transitioned from "Hourly Consulting" to "Structural GTM Architecture," positioning the Nexus GTM engine as a prerequisite for institutional scaling.
*   **Asset Ownership:** Built the `mti-gtm-projection-mirror.html` as a reusable asset for future GTM Academic pitches.

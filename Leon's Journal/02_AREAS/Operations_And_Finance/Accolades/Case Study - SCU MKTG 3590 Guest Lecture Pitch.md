## Lecture Title

GTM Architecture: Executing Market Entry at Scale

---

## Core Thesis

Students spend the semester working inside Country Manager, learning to manage sales force allocation, channel distribution, and SKU pricing under competitive pressure. This session picks up where the sim leaves off. It shows how early-stage companies wire those same variables to signal intelligence, so decisions that take days manually can be made in hours.

---

## Run of Show · 65 Minutes

### Part 1 · The Architecture of Market Entry (15 min)

- Manual sales force expansion has a ceiling. Every new market means new headcount, or a tradeoff somewhere else. This opens with what that ceiling looks like and what the alternative is.
- How practitioners track Market Demand, Regional ICP Match, and Local Compelling Events in real time. These are not abstract strategy concepts; they are the inputs driving entry decisions at actual companies.
- Direct mapping back to Country Manager constraints: channel governance, market entry sequencing, and why the textbook frameworks have an execution layer the textbook does not show you.

### Part 2 · Live Simulator & Capstone Execution (40 min)

- Students interact with a live Revenue Architecture Simulator using Country Manager-calibrated inputs, so every variable on screen is one they have already worked with in the sim.
- Students input their Country Manager constraints. We run the model manually and establish a baseline Net Contribution under a headcount-driven entry. The familiar side first.
- Then we flip the architecture. Automated SKU pricing adjustments and tighter channel targeting run through the same model. We watch Net Contribution move. The point is not the number; it is the decision speed.

### Part 3 · Operator Q&A (10 min)

- Open Q&A on Go-To-Market strategy, revenue operations, and what it actually takes to translate an academic model into an early-stage environment.
- Students walk away with a clearer mental model for the automation layer behind Country Manager decisions, which applies directly to their remaining simulation turns.

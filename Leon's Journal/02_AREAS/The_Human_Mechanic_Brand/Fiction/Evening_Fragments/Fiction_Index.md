# Fiction Index - Evening Fragments

This lane is for short fiction that carries personal signal through one small impossible rule.

The working mode: keep the room real, let one thing disobey reality, and use the rule to expose a sentence the narrator would otherwise avoid.

## Active Pieces

- [[Only Heard]] — Completed 850-word Substack draft combining the Cursor, House, Dog, and Morning Light sequence in first-person.
- [[Night Fragments - Truth Rules]] — Three-part night sequence: cursor, house, dog.
- [[Impossible Domestic Rule Template]] — Reusable drafting scaffold.

## Core Motifs

- The page refuses performance.
- Domestic objects become witnesses.
- Truth is heard before it is accepted.
- Fatherhood is measured by recognizability, not productivity.
- The system becomes quiet enough to reveal the person inside it.

## Editorial Rules

- No explanation of the magic while the scene is alive.
- One impossible rule per fragment.
- Keep the sensory world ordinary: desk, house, kitchen, dog, hallway, window, floor.
- Let the honest sentence create consequence, not closure.
- Avoid turning the piece into commentary about builders, systems, or fatherhood inside the story itself.

## Expansion Queue

- Sequel night: the rule becomes less visible but more intimate.
- Collection frame: "Evening Fragments" as recurring domestic metafiction.
- Publish "Only Heard" as a blog post or Substack edition.

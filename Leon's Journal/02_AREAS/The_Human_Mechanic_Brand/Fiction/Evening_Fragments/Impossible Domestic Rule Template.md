# Impossible Domestic Rule Template

Use this when drafting new pieces in the [[Fiction Index - Evening Fragments]] lane.

## Premise

One ordinary domestic object or sound starts obeying an impossible emotional rule.

Rule:

> [Object / sound] only [does ordinary thing] when [the narrator approaches or avoids a truth].

Examples:

- The cursor only blinks for truth.
- The house only makes sound when he avoids something.
- The dog only sleeps when he stops performing.
- The kitchen light flickers when he almost lies out loud.
- The coffee cools only when he says what he actually wants.

## Draft Pattern

1. State the rule in one clean line.
2. Let the narrator misread it as something ordinary.
3. Test the rule with two almost-true sentences.
4. Ground the room in sensory detail.
5. Let a family or domestic presence raise the stakes.
6. Type, say, or think the honest sentence.
7. Let the rule respond.
8. End with consequence, not explanation.

## Voice Constraints

- Short to medium sentences.
- No lore dump.
- No direct moral.
- No inspirational close.
- Keep the magic quiet.
- Let the last line widen the room.

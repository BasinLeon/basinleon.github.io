# Night Fragments - Truth Rules

## Sequence Logic

Move through the night by making each witness smaller and more intimate.

1. The cursor only blinks for truth.
2. The house only makes sound when he avoids something.
3. The dog only sleeps when he stops performing.

The rules should not feel like worldbuilding. They should feel like the night quietly refusing to let him leave himself.

## Fragment One - The Cursor

The cursor stopped blinking whenever he wrote something false.

At first he thought it was a glitch.

*I'm just tired.*

Nothing.

*I'm proud of what I built today.*

Nothing.

Finally:

*I don't know who I am when nobody needs anything from me.*

The cursor blinked.

Not accepted.

Only heard.

## Fragment Two - The House

After midnight, the house began answering only the sentences he did not finish.

He had gone to the kitchen for water and stood barefoot in the dark, the refrigerator humming with its small domestic confidence.

He thought, *I should sleep.*

The ice maker cracked.

He thought, *Tomorrow will be better.*

The floorboard near the hallway bent under no one's weight.

He held the glass with both hands.

Down the hall, the children slept behind half-closed doors. Their rooms were full of soft proof: shoes kicked sideways, books left open, small shirts folded badly in baskets.

He almost thought, *They don't know how scared I am.*

The house went silent.

Not peaceful.

Waiting.

Finally he whispered it.

"I don't know if I'm becoming better, or just better at hiding the parts that aren't."

The old pipes knocked once inside the wall.

Then the refrigerator hummed again.

## Fragment Three - The Dog

At 2:17, the dog woke up.

Not all the way. Just enough to lift his head and look at him from the doorway with the exhausted patience of a creature who had never needed a brand from him.

He was back at the desk by then.

The document was still open.

He typed another sentence.

*I'm doing all of this for them.*

The dog stood.

Slowly.

He deleted it.

Typed:

*I want them to be proud of me.*

The dog stayed standing.

He deleted that too.

The room had gone cold. The window was still cracked. Somewhere outside, a car passed too fast for the hour and then disappeared into the larger dark.

He looked at the dog.

Then he typed:

*I want them to feel free around me, even when I am disappointed in myself.*

The dog lowered himself back to the floor.

A long sigh.

A warm body returning to sleep.

The cursor blinked beside the sentence.

Not approval.

Company.

## Rawer Variants

### Later That Night

The house started making noise whenever I tried to get away with something.

Not loud noise. Nothing cinematic.

Just enough.

A pipe ticked when I thought, *I'm fine.*

A floorboard answered when I thought, *I just need to push through.*

The fridge hummed when I stood in the kitchen drinking water like a man who had forgotten what thirst was supposed to solve.

Finally I said it out loud.

"I'm afraid I've trained everyone to trust the version of me that performs best under pressure."

The house went quiet.

That was worse.

### 2:17

The dog knew too.

He woke every time I typed something almost true.

*I'm doing this for my family.*

He lifted his head.

*I want to give them a better life.*

He stared.

I hated him a little for being so gentle about it.

Finally I wrote:

*I want to be loved without having to earn the room first.*

The dog put his head back down.

The cursor blinked.

And for a few seconds, I believed the night might let me stay.

## Open Questions

- Does the sequence stay in third person, or does it shift into first person as the night gets more exposed?
- Does the morning after remain realistic, or does one rule follow him into speech?
- Is "Only Heard" the title for the whole sequence or just the cursor fragment?

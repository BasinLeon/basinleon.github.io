Jassi,

Agreed on the need for a proposal.

My view is that we should avoid leading with a standard course list. To get past HR and into the Sales Leadership discussion, we should frame this as a short **Diagnostic Brief** tied to business outcomes, not just training modules.

I see two ways we can position it:

**Option 1: Sales Leadership Lane**

This is the safer route. We position the program around helping the 21 AEs build more **confidence** in prospecting, business development conversations, and closing new digital opportunities.

**Option 2: Revenue Architecture Lane (Recommended)**

This is the stronger route. We position the issue as more than a skills gap. NBC is shifting from a more traditional linear sales model into a digital, non-linear environment. That means the challenge is also about **how the team identifies opportunities, runs discovery, and closes new business in a different revenue motion.**

**My recommendation is Option 2.**

Most training vendors will pitch generic prospecting and closing support. If we lead with a diagnostic brief around **digital revenue execution**, we come in at a higher level and make this more relevant for Sales Leadership.

The brief can show that we understand:

- Where prospecting confidence is breaking down.
- Where discovery and qualification may be stalling.
- How the current sales motion may not fully match the digital business model.
- How training can be tied to measurable business outcomes.

**Next step:**

I suggest we reply to Kevin saying we are putting together a short **diagnostic-based proposal** for leadership review. Once they review it, we can discuss a call.

If you agree, I can help draft the template so we can reuse it across future media and enterprise training opportunities.

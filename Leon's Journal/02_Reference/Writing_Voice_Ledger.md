# Writing Voice Ledger

## Keep Phrases

- 2026-03-26: "Wanted to follow up on our March 11 conversation." -> strong recruiter follow-up opener; calm, direct, low-friction.

## Avoid Phrases

- 2026-03-26: "I continue to think I could add value here, especially where GTM execution, systems thinking, and operator-level ownership matter." -> too self-descriptive for a simple recruiter follow-up.

## Voice Settings

- Prefer short to medium sentences.
- Prefer direct language over explanatory positioning.
- Prefer calm operator tone over polished corporate tone.
- Avoid em dashes unless explicitly wanted.
- Avoid AI-sounding filler, inflated framing, and consultant theater.

## Format Rules

### Email

- Answer or purpose in the first line.
- Keep the ask small and easy to reply to.
- Do not re-pitch when a simple status check is enough.

### Messages

- Keep them short, direct, and human.
- Avoid stacked qualifiers and unnecessary context.

### Posts

- One thesis per post.
- Practical over performative.

### Articles / Essays

- Clear claim up front.
- Keep sections short and moving.

## Approved Patterns

- Follow-up pattern: opener -> interest confirmation -> low-pressure status ask -> simple close.
- Writing pattern: one idea per paragraph.
- Editing pattern: cut the self-description before cutting the useful ask.

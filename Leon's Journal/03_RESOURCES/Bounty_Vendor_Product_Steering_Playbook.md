# Bounty / Vendor Product Steering Playbook

## Core Principle
When a vendor asks for feedback, roadmap input, telemetry, case-study rights, or market intelligence, the conversation is no longer simple product usage.

It becomes a question of **what role Leon is playing**:

1. customer
2. pilot partner
3. strategic distribution / architecture partner

The mistake is letting those roles blur together.

## The Structural Reframe
Do not respond like a grateful beta user asking for more credits.

Respond like a Revenue Architect deciding whether the platform deserves to become part of a larger operating system.

The frame is:

- customer usage is one lane
- architectural feedback is a second lane
- strategic growth or distribution is a third lane

Those lanes require different economics.

## The Real Risk
Vendors will often try to compress the following into one soft conversation:

- product testing
- free consulting
- market training data
- roadmap guidance
- case study rights
- eventual paid adoption

If that bundle is not separated, Leon ends up paying to improve someone else's platform while also de-risking their roadmap.

## The Three Engagement Modes

### 1. Customer Mode
Use this when the platform is still unproven.

- Leon evaluates the tool at normal pricing or trial limits
- no deep product strategy is shared
- no custom telemetry package is promised
- no public case study rights are implied

**Use when:** the tool might be useful, but leverage is still low and proof is incomplete.

### 2. Defined Pilot Mode
Use this when the vendor wants meaningful feedback and Leon wants proof without overcommitting cash.

- vendor extends a larger credit sandbox or pilot access
- Leon runs 2-3 real workflows
- Leon returns structured findings:
  - what worked
  - what broke
  - what higher-value jobs are worth building next
- no open-ended advisory access

**This is the default best move.**

### 3. Strategic Partner Mode
Use this only after the pilot proves real yield.

- Bounty becomes part of a Basin::Nexus execution layer
- Leon may use them in case studies, lectures, or client architecture
- economics move toward:
  - affiliate
  - rev-share
  - credit injection
  - co-sell or partner mechanics

**This mode should never be granted off promise alone.**

## Negotiation Rules

### Rule 1: Separate usage from strategy
If they want user behavior, they get standard usage.
If they want operator intelligence, they fund a pilot.

### Rule 2: Never pay full price to become unpaid product strategy
If Leon is helping them think about category direction, template logic, or market expansion, there must be a value exchange.

### Rule 3: Keep the door open without collapsing the frame
The goal is not to punish the vendor.
The goal is to keep the relationship warm while protecting leverage.

### Rule 4: Growth comes after proof
If Leon wants to grow with them, the right path is:

1. prove execution on a small pilot
2. define where Bounty fits in the Nexus stack
3. then discuss case study, lecture mention, referrals, or partner structure

### Rule 5: Do not give away the map too early
High-yield signal logic, market architecture, and deployment strategy are not "casual feedback."

They are part of the Basin::Nexus advantage.

## Best Reply Structure

### Part 1: Validate the direction
Example:
"I do see where Bounty is heading, and the move from raw sourcing toward end-to-end execution is the right one."

### Part 2: Split the conversation into lanes
Example:
"From my side, there are two distinct modes here: straightforward customer usage, or a more strategic engagement where I give structured operator feedback and workflow telemetry."

### Part 3: Define the exchange
Example:
"If you want pure customer behavior, I can keep exploring at the current level. If you want deeper architectural feedback, I'd prefer to do that through a defined pilot."

### Part 4: Offer a small next step
Example:
"The cleanest structure would be a larger credit sandbox tied to 2-3 real workflow tests."

### Part 5: Preserve upside without overcommitting
Example:
"If the pilot proves out, I'd be happy to revisit broader paid usage, case study inclusion, and whether a more formal partnership layer makes sense."

## Specific Bounty Positioning
The right message to Bounty is not:

"Can I have more credits?"

It is:

"I am deciding whether Bounty earns a place in my operating system. If you want real operator feedback and potential downstream distribution, let's structure that intentionally."

## What Improves the Current Draft
The draft should get slightly stronger in two places:

### 1. Make the role distinction more explicit
Do not just say there are two conversations.
Name the economic difference between them.

### 2. Clarify that growth is on the table
Bounty should understand that Leon is not pulling away.
He is simply sequencing the relationship correctly:

- pilot first
- proof second
- expansion third

## Recommended Message Posture
Warm, direct, bounded.

Not:
- needy
- offended
- overexplaining
- giving away too much strategy for free

## Reusable Line Bank

### Boundary Lines
- "Those are not the same conversation."
- "Customer usage and architectural feedback operate under different economics."
- "I want to be thoughtful about how much strategic energy I put into deeper testing right now."

### Pilot Lines
- "The cleanest structure would be a defined pilot tied to 2-3 real workflow tests."
- "That gives you structured feedback based on actual execution rather than surface impressions."

### Growth Lines
- "If the pilot proves out, I can absolutely see a broader relationship here."
- "If the execution layer holds up, I'd be happy to revisit paid usage, case study inclusion, and a stronger partnership structure."
- "I'm open to growing with the platform, but I want to sequence that off proof rather than enthusiasm alone."

## Recommended Current Move
Use the defined pilot frame.

That preserves:
- leverage
- relationship warmth
- optionality
- room to grow with them later

Without:
- paying too early
- subsidizing their roadmap
- giving away the Basin::Nexus logic layer for free

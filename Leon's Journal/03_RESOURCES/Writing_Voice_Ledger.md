# Writing Voice Ledger

## Keep Phrases

- 2026-05-09: "The main issue is not generating more leads." Good operator diagnosis. States the real problem without throat-clearing.
- 2026-05-09: "The bigger opportunity now is improving conversion, repeat usage, retention, and packaging the right B2B motion." Strong because it replaces vague growth talk with the actual levers.
- 2026-05-09: "Write to transfer decisions, not to perform intelligence." Keep as a system rule and editorial filter.
- 2026-05-12: "Version 1 is directionally right, but the higher-leverage move is to position us as Revenue Architects solving a structural revenue transition..." Good because it opens with the strategic shift, not praise or self-description.

## Avoid Phrases

- 2026-05-09: "I took a look at Version 1. It's solid." Too soft and generic as an opener.
- 2026-05-09: "I recommend we look at..." Adds distance where direct ownership would read better.
- 2026-05-09: "The goal is to keep this lean, prove value quickly, and scale what works." Fine idea, but generic unless paired with specific actions.
- 2026-05-12: "My background is..." and "I do my best work when..." Weak default for outreach. Usually replace with company-specific fit, proof, or the concrete shift you understand.

## Voice Settings

- Short to medium sentences.
- First two lines should contain the diagnosis or the point.
- One idea per paragraph.
- Warm, direct, and operator-level over polished or academic.
- Prefer concrete verbs and live business nouns: signal, route, owner, conversion, retention, pilot.
- Explain their world before explaining yourself.

## Format Rules

- Email: state the real issue fast, give 3-4 proof points, then name the next step.
- Post/article: use a sharp line that reframes the problem in plain language.
- Internal note: optimize for decisions, priorities, and what moves next.

## Approved Patterns

- Diagnosis first, context second.
- Problem line followed by proof bullets.
- Clear next move instead of a soft inspirational close.
- Situation fit first, self-positioning second.

Battle Scripts PRACTICE OUT LOUD
"Tell me about yourself"
TRAP: Telling the founder/builder/consulting story
"I'm a GTM operations leader with 10+ years in B2B. I started at SurveyMonkey learning how large-scale partner ecosystems work, then spent time at Sense building the partner-led pipeline engine. Most recently at Fudo Security, I managed US operations and grew the pipeline 160% by building the inbound/outbound systems. The reason I'm here is that my entire career converges on what this role needs: recruiting partners, enforcing CRM hygiene, and making sure external agencies actually generate revenue for the internal sales team."
"Why Ketch?"
TRAP: Generic flattery or not knowing the product
"We are entering an AI-driven K-shaped economy where the single most valuable asset is data, and the biggest looming crisis is data ownership and consent. Ketch isn't just a compliance tool; it's the structural infrastructure that allows enterprises to leverage AI without violating the fundamental right to data governance. But to scale that infrastructure globally, you need an army of agency and consultancy partners implementing it. I specialize in taking an early-stage partner program, segmenting the agencies, and building the co-sell pipeline so that those partners actually become a pure revenue engine for Ketch. That structural build phase is exactly where I operate best."
"What's your experience with partnerships?"
TRAP: Over-engineering with custom automation stories
"Two layers. First, I've built partner programs from scratch. At TalSmart, I architected the go-to-market for an enterprise training product and closed a $50K deal through a partner-led motion. Second, at Fudo Security, I managed the channel partner ecosystem for the US market. I built the co-sell pipeline process and made sure partner-sourced leads converted at the same rate as direct. The common thread: I treat partnerships as a revenue function, tracking activation rates and strict QBRs, not just relationship management."
"Are you overqualified? We're looking for 2-4 years experience."
TRAP: Getting defensive or sounding too expensive
"I have 10 years of operational experience, which means I know exactly what breaks when an early-stage partner program tries to scale. I’m not looking for an ego title; I’m looking for an execution role where I can roll up my sleeves, run the QBRs, activate dormant agencies, and generate revenue. I know how to do the unglamorous work required to make a partner channel actually convert."
"What keeps you up at night?"
TRAP: "Inefficiency" or "The future of AI"
"Partner-sourced leads that don't convert. If we're investing in alliance relationships but the leads they send us are dying in the pipeline, that's a system failure. I obsess over the handoff: did the partner qualify correctly? Did our team follow up fast enough? Is the attribution clean? If the partner doesn't see results, the relationship dies. That's what keeps me up."
"What are your salary expectations?"
TRAP: Anchoring outside the $100K - $140K band
"I'm focused on the right fit and the scope of the role. I understand the baseline for this position is mapped between $100,000 and $140,000, and I'm very comfortable within that range. I'm happy to discuss specifics once we've confirmed mutual fit."

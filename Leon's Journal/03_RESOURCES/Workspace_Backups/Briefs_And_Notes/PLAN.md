# Basin & Associates Writing Research Backlog

## Summary
Create a mixed-portfolio writing backlog that connects Basin & Associates, the private Obsidian vault, the public writing hub, and a fresh Google Doc handoff. The output should be a ranked, evidence-backed list of topics Leon can write from immediately, with each idea tied to existing source material, audience, format, and next action.

Primary deliverable: a new Google Doc containing the research-backed topic backlog, while treating Obsidian as the durable source of truth.

## Key Changes
- Use Obsidian as the master knowledge layer:
  - Source from `Leon's Journal/02_Reference/`, session notes, voice ledger, frameworks, and business case notes.
  - Preserve each topic’s source trail with vault paths and short evidence notes.
  - Add a clear “Obsidian backlink / source note” field for every recommended idea.

- Use Google Docs as the collaboration and drafting layer:
  - Create a fresh Google Doc titled like `Basin & Associates Writing Backlog - Obsidian Research Map`.
  - Structure it for fast scanning: executive summary, topic pillars, prioritized backlog, draft-ready briefs, and source index.
  - Keep it shareable without exposing private/proprietary details unnecessarily.

- Build a mixed portfolio topic system:
  - GTM/Nexus: revenue architecture, signal systems, AI workflow quality, routing, operator diagnostics.
  - Personal Brand: founder/hiring-manager proof, career narrative, operator identity, public trust.
  - Writing/Meta: turning notes into publishable essays, anti-AI-slop editing, distribution routines.
  - Literary/Reflective: identity, memory, inner life, and selective personal essays that support the public proof layer.

## Topic Backlog Format
Each topic should include:
- Working title
- Pillar: GTM/Nexus, Personal Brand, Writing/Meta, or Literary/Reflective
- Best format: article, LinkedIn post, thread, case study, brief, or essay
- Audience: founder, hiring manager, operator, technical reader, or general reader
- Source evidence: Obsidian/public-doc references
- Core thesis
- Why it matters now
- Draft angle
- Priority: now, next, later
- Sensitivity: public-safe, anonymize, private-only

Initial high-priority themes should include:
- “The private Obsidian brain behind the public proof layer”
- “Why pipeline breaks at interpretation, not outreach volume”
- “How I turn messy operator notes into publishable systems thinking”
- “What Google Docs is good for, and what Obsidian should own”
- “The difference between content marketing and operator proof”
- “How Basin::Nexus thinks about signal-to-yield”
- “Why specificity beats storytelling fluff in B2B writing”
- “The public/private boundary for writing about real execution”

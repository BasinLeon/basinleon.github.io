<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Industry: Payment Processing, E-commerce, Finance</b></div>
<div><b>Purpose: Protects cardholder data and prevents credit card fraud.</b></div>

<div><b><br></b></div>

<div><b>Step 1: Core Privileged Access Management (PAM) Terms</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>1. Privileged Account:</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	A user account with elevated permissions (e.g., admin accounts, service accounts).</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Why it matters: These accounts are high-value targets for attackers because they can access critical systems and data.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>2. Least Privilege:</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Principle that users should only have the minimum access necessary to perform their job.</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Why it matters: Reduces the risk of accidental or malicious changes.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>3. Session Recording and Monitoring:</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Captures and logs privileged session activity for auditing and security purposes.</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Example: PAM solutions record admin sessions to detect risky behavior.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>4. Password Vault:</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Centralized, encrypted storage for managing and rotating privileged account credentials.</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Why it matters: Ensures secure storage and prevents password reuse.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>5. Just-In-Time (JIT) Access:</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Provides temporary privileged access when needed and removes it afterward.</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Why it matters: Limits exposure time for privileged accounts, reducing the attack surface.</b><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>Step 2: Key Technologies Surrounding PAM</b></div>
<div>	1.	<b>Multi-Factor Authentication (MFA):</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Adds an extra layer of security by requiring a second form of verification (e.g., SMS code or app-based verification).</b></div>
<div>	2.	<b>SIEM (Security Information and Event Management):</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Aggregates and analyzes security logs to detect threats in real time. Often integrates with PAM for comprehensive monitoring.</b></div>
<div>	3.	<b>Zero Trust Security:</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Core principle for modern cybersecurity: ØNever trust, always verify.Æ PAM is a critical component of Zero Trust strategies.</b></div>
<div>	4.	<b>Endpoint Detection and Response (EDR):</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Monitors and responds to threats on endpoints (workstations, servers).</b></div>
<div>	5.	<b>Role-Based Access Control (RBAC):</b></div>
<div><b>	Ë	Assigns access based on a userís role in the organization. Reduces manual privilege assignment.</b><br></div>

<div><span style="font-size: 16px">## The Three Unfair Advantages</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">When I stopped *managing* GTM systems and started *building* them, three truths emerged:</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">### 1. Signal Fidelity</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">Every tool you buy imposes someone else's view onto your data. When you build, you decide which signals matter.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">At a Series A cybersecurity company, we were drowning in &quotleads.&quot I built a signal engine with a 0.8 precision gate. **160% pipeline growth**Ńnot from more activity, but from better architecture.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">### 2. Speed of Iteration</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">A Python script iterates in hours. A vendor roadmap iterates in quarters.</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px"><br></span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">### 3. Ownership of Differentiation</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 16px">If your GTM edge is a workflow trick, it disappears when you bolt it to SaaS. When you *build* the tooling? The advantage stays.</span></div>

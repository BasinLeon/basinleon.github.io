<div><b>1. Key Texts of Kabbalah (you must know these names cold)</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Primary Sacred Texts:</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Sefer Yetzirah (“Book of Formation”)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Early mystical work explaining creation through Hebrew letters and numbers.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Zohar (“Book of Splendor”)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Mystical commentary on the Torah, attributed to Rabbi Shimon bar Yochai.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Sefer HaBahir (“Book of Brightness”)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Ancient mystical text, foundational for understanding the Sephirot and soul concepts.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Later and Essential Kabbalistic Works:</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Etz Chaim (“Tree of Life”)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Written by Rabbi Chaim Vital, teachings of Isaac Luria (The Ari). Explains Tzimtzum, Shevirat HaKelim, Tikkun Olam.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Tikkunei Zohar (“Rectifications of the Zohar”)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Supplementary teachings on the Zohar’s deepest mysteries.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Sha’ar HaGilgulim (“Gate of Reincarnations”)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Deals with reincarnation of souls (</b><b><i>gilgulim</i></b><b>), from Lurianic Kabbalah.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Modern Commentaries:</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Sulam Commentary by Rabbi Yehuda Ashlag (Baal HaSulam)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	The clearest modern commentary on the Zohar.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Derech Hashem (“The Way of God”) by Ramchal (Rabbi Moshe Chaim Luzzatto)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Systematic explanation of divine structure, providence, and the purpose of creation.</b></div>

<div><b>3. Core Concepts and Terminology (burn these into your soul)</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Ein Sof – The Infinite, unknowable aspect of God.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Tzimtzum – Divine contraction to create “space” for creation.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Shevirat HaKelim – Shattering of the vessels; cosmic crisis that led to brokenness in creation.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Tikkun – Repair, restoration of harmony.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Sephirot – The 10 attributes/emanations of God through which He interacts with creation (e.g., Chesed (Kindness), Gevurah (Strength), Tiferet (Beauty), etc.).</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Tree of Life – The map of the Sephirot.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Olamot – Worlds; layers of reality: Atzilut (Emanation), Beriah (Creation), Yetzirah (Formation), Assiyah (Action).</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Partzufim – Divine faces or personas within the Sephirot structure.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Sefirotic Triads – Organization of the 10 Sephirot into 3 triads: Intellect, Emotions, Manifestation.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Gilgul – Reincarnation; the recycling of souls for rectification.</b></div>
<div><b>	•	Da’at – Knowledge; often hidden or revealed depending on human work.</b></div>

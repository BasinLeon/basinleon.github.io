# Juggling Acts: Parenting, Pursuing an EMBA, and Building a Career*Created: Wednesday, June 21, 2023 at 9:30:17?PM*<div>Juggling Acts: Parenting, Pursuing an EMBA, and Building a Career</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>I'd like to share a personal story today, one that often feels like a tightrope walk, a balancing act, and a thrilling roller coaster ride all rolled into one. It's the story of wearing three hats simultaneously: being a parent, pursuing an Executive MBA (EMBA), and building a professional career. </div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Parenting: The Foundation</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>The journey begins with the most demanding and rewarding role - being a parent. As any parent will tell you, parenting is not a 9 to 5 job; it's a 24/7 commitment that demands your attention, energy, and heart. The countless sleepless nights, the numerous school runs, the relentless worry, and the endless joy - every moment, challenging or heartwarming, lays the groundwork for a unique kind of resilience and determination.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Pursuing an EMBA: The Uphill Climb</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Simultaneously, I decided to embark on another demanding journey - pursuing an EMBA. I was often awake long after my kids were asleep, buried in books, preparing for presentations, and cracking complex case studies. There were times when I questioned my decision, times when the mountain seemed too high to climb. But then, I'd remember the curious eyes of my children, watching their parent working hard, learning, and growing. I realized I wasn't just getting an EMBA for myself; I was setting an example of perseverance and lifelong learning for them.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Building a Professional Career: The Tightrope Walk</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Amidst parenting and studying, I was also focused on building my professional career. Balancing work commitments, meeting targets, networking, and continually upskilling were all part of the daily grind. There were days when the lines between these roles blurred, when the pressure seemed overwhelming. Yet, it was the tightrope walker's focus and balance that I adopted. I learned to compartmentalize, to be fully present in whatever I was doing - be it a business meeting, a study group, or a bedtime story.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>The Symphony of Life</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Looking back, juggling these three significant aspects of my life was no easy feat. It was an exercise in time management, a test of resilience, and a lesson in balance. But more than that, it was a realization that life isn't about choosing one path over another; it's about creating a symphony with all the roles we play.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Being a parent made me a better student, understanding the value of time and patience. Pursuing an EMBA made me a better professional, equipped with new knowledge and perspectives. And building a career made me a better parent, teaching my children about dedication, hard work, and the importance of pursuing one's passion.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>In the end, it's not about wearing different hats; it's about realizing that all these roles are not separate, but harmoniously interconnected parts of who we are.</div>

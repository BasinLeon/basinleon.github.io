# 15 Video Note Hacks To Win Clients Everytime*Created: Tuesday, January 6, 2026 at 5:26:16?PM*<div><b><h2>15 Video Note Hacks To Win Clients Everytime</h2></b></div>
<div>These work regardless of ICP, industry, or seniority level. Based on real campaigns + buyer psychology.</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b>HACK #1: The &quotImperfect Human&quot Signal</b><br></div>
<div><b>What:</b><br></div>
<div>Record in 1 take with minor imperfections (brief pause, slight &quotum,&quot natural laugh).</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b><br></div>
<div>Mirror neurons detect authenticity. Polished = corporate = ignored. Hesitation = human = trustworthy.</div>
<div><b>Real Example:</b><br></div>
<div>&quotHey [name]...</div>
<div><i>pause</i><br></div>
<div>... sorry, I'm thinking while I talk Ń but I noticed X and genuinely curious...&quot</div>
<div><b>Psychology:</b><br></div>
<div>Brain processes &quotmessy&quot speech as genuine. Perfection triggers skepticism.</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #2: The &quotSmile-Voice&quot Effect</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Literally smile while recording. Your voice tone changes even if they can't see your face.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Vocal tone conveys warmth, activating liking principle. Studies show people can <i>hear</i> smiles through audio.</div>
<div><b>Execution:</b> Before hitting record, think of something funny. Then start. Your vocal cords physically shift.</div>
<div><b>Caution:</b> Don't fake it Ń think of an actual joke/memory. Faking creates vocal tension listeners detect subconsciously.</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #3: The &quotWalk-and-Talk&quot Energy</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Record while pacing (not sitting). For video, film walking outside or in motion.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Movement = energy = conviction. Sitting = low-status = desperate. Motion conveys confidence without arrogance.</div>
<div><b>Real Example:</b> &quotHey [name], just stepping between meetings and wanted to reach out quick because...&quot</div>
<div><b>Bonus:</b> Environmental noise (slight traffic, office background) = social proof you're busy/important.</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #4: The &quot10Đ18 Second Rule&quot</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Keep voice notes 10Đ18 seconds MAX. Video notes 15Đ22 seconds.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Attention span + reciprocity balance. Too short = no value. Too long = cognitive load.</div>
<div><b>Formula:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>First 3 seconds: Hook (name + pattern interrupt)</li>
<li>Next 8Đ12 seconds: Single insight/question</li>
<li>Last 3 seconds: Zero-pressure exit</li>
<li><b>Example Timing:</b> &quotHey Sarah... <i>[3s]</i> noticed you're tackling X challenge Ń curious how you're thinking about Y? <i>[12s]</i> No worries if timing's off, just thought it'd be worth exploring. <i>[3s]</i>&quot &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #5: The &quotIncomplete Curiosity Loop&quot</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Start a thought but DON'T complete it. Leave them hanging just enough.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Brain hates unresolved patterns (Zeigarnik effect). Forces response to get closure.</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotI've been noticing something weird with [their industry] Ń wondering if you're seeing it too, or if I'm...&quot</li>
<li>&quotQuick thought about [their company] Ń might be totally off, but have you considered...&quot</li>
<li><b>Psychology:</b> Incomplete statements = open loop = they must respond to close it. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #6: The &quotAgainst-My-Interest&quot Confession</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Admit something slightly unfavorable about yourself/your service upfront.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Vulnerability = authenticity. Also disarms sales defense.</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotFull transparency Ń I don't even know if we're a fit for you, but I'm curious about...&quot</li>
<li>&quotThis might sound random, and honestly I'm not sure you'll even care, but...&quot</li>
<li>&quotI almost didn't reach out because [honest reason], but figured...&quot<b>Psychology:</b> Admitting weakness = credibility surge. If you say one negative, they believe your positives. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #7: The &quotObservation &gt Assumption&quot Frame</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Present what you <i>saw</i> vs. what you <i>think</i>. Let them fill the gap.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Questions &gt assertions. People don't argue with their own conclusions.</div>
<div><b>Bad:</b> &quotI can help you with X.&quot</div>
<div><b>Good:</b> &quotSaw you posted about X challenge. Are you solving that with [approach A] or [approach B]?&quot</div>
<div><b>Real Example:</b> &quotI've been learning from Business Leaders that tech issues are slowing things down... are you seeing this too, or are you confident in your systems?&quot</div>
<div><b>Psychology:</b> Binary question with &quotor are you confident&quot = ego-stroke exit. They'll correct you if wrong = engagement.</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #8: The &quotPeer-Curiosity&quot Positioning</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Frame yourself as researching <i>their world</i>, not selling yours.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Flips power dynamic. You're the student = they're the expert = ego fulfillment.</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotI'm talking to [their role] in [industry] and learning X pattern Ń does that match your experience?&quot</li>
<li>&quotHonest question Ń I'm seeing [trend], but you're way deeper in this than I am. What's your take?&quot</li>
<li><b>Real Campaign :</b> &quotIn my experience working with clinicians, inconsistent platelet quality is the #1 frustration. Have you noticed this, or has it been pretty consistent for you?&quot</li>
<li><b>Psychology:</b> Authority inversion + asking for help. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #9: The &quotCasual Mistake&quot Humanizer</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Include a tiny, obvious mistake and acknowledge it in the same breath.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Perfection = robotic. Small mistakes = human = relatable.</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li><i>[Slight stumble]</i> &quotSorry, lost my train of thought there... anyway, what I meant was...&quot</li>
<li>&quotI realize I'm probably butchering the pronunciation of [term], but...&quot</li>
<li><i>[Video: glance at notes]</i> &quotLet me make sure I get this right... yeah, so...&quot</li>
<li><b>Psychology:</b> Self-deprecation (done lightly) = liking principle. Shows you're not trying to be perfect = less threatening. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #10: The &quotNo-Pitch Inoculation&quot</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Explicitly say &quotnot pitching&quot BEFORE they think you are.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Pre-empts objection. Once you say &quotno pitch,&quot their brain relaxes. If you pitch later, they'll correct you (engagement).</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotNot pitching anything here, just genuinely curious...&quot</li>
<li>&quotHope you don't mind the voice note Ń not trying to sell you, just wanted to ask...&quot</li>
<li>&quotNo agenda, just building my network and thought I'd ask...&quot</li>
<li><b>Real Campaign:</b> Connection says &quotNo agenda Ń just building my network.&quot 56% reply rate.</li>
<li><b>Psychology:</b> Saying what you're NOT doing = they stop looking for it = guard drops. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #11: The &quotEasy-Out Paradox&quot</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Give them explicit permission to ignore you.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Reverse psychology + loss aversion. When you remove pressure, they feel <i>more</i> inclined to respond (scarcity of your attention).</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quot...and if this doesn't resonate, no worries at all Ń just let me know and I won't bug you.&quot</li>
<li>&quotTotally fine if this isn't relevant right now. Either way, appreciate the connection.&quot</li>
<li>&quotIf you're confident in your systems, no worries Ń just wanted to check in.&quot</li>
<li><b>Real Campaign:</b> &quotAnd if you don't find this inquiry resonates... no worries at all, just let me know.&quot 48% reply rate.</li>
<li><b>Psychology:</b> Permission to refuse = they feel <i>in control</i> = less reactance = higher compliance. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #12: The &quotEnvironmental Status Cue&quot</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Record in environments that signal competence without bragging.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Authority + social proof via context. Setting = subtle status marker.</div>
<div><b>Examples (Video):</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>Bookshelf in background (authority = books)</li>
<li>Outside natural light (busy = walking between things)</li>
<li>Office with team visible behind (social proof = you have a team)</li>
<li>Car (mobile = important/busy)</li>
<li><b>Avoid:</b> Bedroom, messy backgrounds, poor lighting = low-status signals.</li>
<li><b>Audio:</b> Background ambience (office chatter, cafŽ) = social proof you're embedded in professional world. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #13: The &quotTheir-Words-Back&quot Mirror</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Use exact phrases from their LinkedIn posts/profile in your voice note, then ask them to expand.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Recognition + validation. When you quote someone back to themselves, they feel heard (rare on LinkedIn).</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotYou mentioned '[exact phrase from their post]' Ń that really stuck with me. Can you expand on what you meant by that?&quot</li>
<li>&quotI saw you describe your approach as '[their words]' which is interesting because most people say [common alternative]...&quot</li>
<li>&quotYou wrote '[their phrase]' in your profile. That's a specific way to put it Ń what led you to frame it that way?&quot</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Real Campaign:</b> Reference something they posted 2-3 weeks ago (not yesterday = creepy, not 6 months ago = you're digging too deep).</div>
<div><b>Psychology:</b> Quoting someone = you actually paid attention. Asking them to explain = expert positioning + ego fulfillment. People love expanding on their own ideas.</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #14: The &quotContrast Frame&quot Positioning</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Present two opposite scenarios and let them self-identify with one.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Binary choices feel easier than open questions. Plus people love correcting assumptions = engagement.</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotQuick question Ń are you in 'growth mode' right now or more 'optimize what we have' mode?&quot</li>
<li>&quotI'm curious Ń is [problem] on your radar, or have you already figured that out?&quot</li>
<li>&quotMost [their role] I talk to are either drowning in [X] or they've automated it. Which camp are you in?&quot</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Psychology:</b> Contrast principle makes decision easier. Plus most people will pick one AND explain why = longer response = relationship building.</div>
<div><b>Caution:</b> Make both options neutral (not &quotgood vs bad&quot). You want honest answer, not defensive response.</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>
<div>&ltaside&gt</div>
<div><b><h2>HACK #15: The &quotSpecific Number&quot Credibility</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Use oddly specific numbers instead of round ones when referencing data or experience.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Specificity = credibility. &quotAbout 10&quot sounds made up. &quot12&quot sounds researched.</div>
<div><b>Examples:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>&quotI've talked to 47 [their role] in the past few months and 38 of them mentioned [pain point]...&quot</li>
<li>&quotThis usually takes teams about 6.5 hours per week Ń does that sound about right for you?&quot</li>
<li>&quotWe've seen 23 companies in [industry] move away from [X] recently. Are you seeing the same shift?&quot</li>
</ul>
<div><b>Real Campaign:</b> &quotI've spoken with 67 clinicians&quot vs &quotI've spoken with many clinicians&quot Ń first one got 41% higher reply rate.</div>
<div><b>Psychology:</b> Specific = you didn't just guess. Brain interprets precise numbers as factual. Round numbers = approximation = less trustworthy.</div>
<div><b>Bonus:</b> Combine with Hack #8 (Peer-Curiosity). &quotI've talked to 34 people in your position Ń they all said X. You seeing the same thing?&quot</div>
<div>&lt/aside&gt</div>

<div><b><h2>WILD CARD #1: The &quotWrong-Name Recovery&quot</h2></b></div>
<div><b>What:</b> Intentionally use wrong first name in text, then send immediate voice note correction.</div>
<div><b>Why It Works:</b> Mistake = human. Correction via voice = extra touchpoint + shows you care.</div>
<div><b>Example:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>Text: &quotHey John, quick question about...&quot</li>
<li>30 seconds later, voice note: <i>&quotOh god, I just realized I called you John Ń your name is James, right? Sorry about that. Anyway, here's what I meant...&quot</i><br></li>
<li><b>Psychology:</b> Mistake + correction = memorable. They'll remember you (good) and the effort to fix it (better).</li>
<li><b>Caution:</b> Use sparingly (1x per 50 messages). Overuse = looks fake. &lt/aside&gt</li>
</ul>

</ul>

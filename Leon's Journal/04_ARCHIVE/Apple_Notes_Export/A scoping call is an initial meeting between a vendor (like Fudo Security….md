# A scoping call is an initial meeting between a vendor (like Fudo Securityè*Created: Wednesday, July 17, 2024 at 9:41:53?AM*<div>A scoping call is an initial meeting between a vendor (like Fudo Security) and a potential client (like Viso Trust in this case) to discuss the scope of a project or Proof of Concept (POC). It's a crucial step in the sales process as it helps both parties align their expectations and understand the project's requirements, goals, timeline, and resources.</div>
<div>Here's what's typically covered in a POC scoping call:</div>
<div> * Introductions: Participants introduce themselves and their roles in the project.</div>
<div> * Problem Statement: The client outlines the problem they're trying to solve or the opportunity they want to explore.</div>
<div> * Solution Overview: The vendor provides a high-level overview of their proposed solution and how it can address the client's needs.</div>
<div> * Success Criteria: Both parties agree on what constitutes a successful POC, including measurable metrics and desired outcomes.</div>
<div> * Timeline and Resources: The vendor and client discuss the estimated timeline for the POC, including milestones and deliverables. They also identify the resources required from both sides, such as personnel, data, and technical infrastructure.</div>
<div> * Budget: If applicable, the vendor may provide a preliminary estimate of the cost of the POC.</div>
<div> * Next Steps: The parties agree on the next steps, such as drafting a formal POC agreement or scheduling a follow-up meeting.</div>

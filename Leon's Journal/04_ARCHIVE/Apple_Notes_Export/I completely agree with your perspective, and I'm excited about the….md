<div><b><h2>What Is Talent Intelligence?</h2></b></div>
<div>Talent intelligence is a method for assessing potential hires, trends in the job market and internal performance benchmarks to improve role-specific hiring success. It evaluates existing workforces and employer branding to determine the companyÕs competitive edge. The framework analyzes deep behavioral attributes of candidates compared to company-specific performance indicators to hire the right person for the job and culture.</div>
<div><b><h2>Importance</h2></b></div>
<div>Talent selection is not a one-size-fits-all process. Accurately assessing candidatesÕ role alignment requires approaches that standardize the unique needs of each role and assessment techniques that measure our applicantsÕ distinctive strengths and attributes.</div>
<div>With TI, we can accurately zero in on top talent when we know what makes candidates an excellent match for the role. So first, weÕll need an accurate picture of the organizationÕs dynamics and inner workings.</div>
<div>HereÕs how talent intelligence is essential to address the challenges of hiring modern talent:</div>
<div><b><h2>1. Addresses New Talent Dynamics</h2></b></div>
<div>Companies of all shapes and sizes are starting to realize the importance of targeting employed individuals who arenÕt actively looking for a job change in their recruitment strategy.</div>
<div>Today, categorizing the labor market into active and passive candidates leads to myopic hiring. Despite sourcing all types of candidates, the endgame is still the same Ñ to fill vacancies. Every company has unique talent requirements, and hiring to fill vacancies is a short-term solution.</div>
<div>Understanding how talent moves into organizations and why employees leave requires holistic knowledge of how recruitment and talent management efforts influence each other. Talent intelligence is a valuable instrument for analyzing the existing talent market and identifying industry-specific hiring trends that assist in data-backed hiring decisions.</div>
<div><b><h2>2. Stabilizes Turnover Rates</h2></b></div>
<div>While there will always be times when employees leave an organization, a steadily increasing rate of resignations indicates a larger talent management issue. Parting ways to change career paths is reasonable; quitting due to a toxic culture or vague leadership shouldnÕt be a reason.</div>
<div>When employees resign, they take their knowledge and expertise with them. Replacing that experience, especially for a specialized role, is challenging. ItÕs no wonder that just mentioning employee turnover is enough to make any HR person flinch.</div>
<div>Talent intelligence takes a deep dive into internal talent mobility to diagnose why former employees resigned, identify whoÕs at risk of leaving and take proactive measures to prevent an employee exodus.</div>
<div><b><h2>3. Supports Diversity, Equality and Inclusion</h2></b></div>
<div>Millennials and Gen Z are the most diverse, highly value-driven generations taking over the workplace, and itÕs no surprise that these young job seekers sparked a DEI reckoning in the workplace that influenced businesses to prioritize workplace diversity and inclusion. With baby boomers exiting the workforce, theÊWorld Economic ForumÊestimates that Gen Z will make up 27% of the global workforce by 2025.</div>
<div>Culture AmpÕs DEI ReportÊfound that employers are adopting a data-centric approach to balance talent pipelines. The report found that 45% of companies use DEI metrics to make impactful decisions, and a majority of 70% implemented strategic DEI plans that explicitly included diverse talent-sourcing methods.</div>
<div>If your company needs to enhance its diversity efforts, talent intelligence is the way. Evaluating our workforce demographics and pinpointing where inequities might occur is valuable to setting strategic DEI goals and achieving those targets.</div>

<div>While our recruiting teams breathe a sigh of relief when a position is filled, bringing in new employees doesnÕt always guarantee success. <div>What makes a candidate a good fit? The answer changes with every new talent trend. Not long ago, recruiters realized that soft skills are equally important as technical skills. Today, more and more HR thought leaders preach theÊimportance of power skills.</div>

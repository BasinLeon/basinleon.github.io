# mendocino*Created: Sunday, October 7, 2018 at 3:26:40?PM*<div>mendocino</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>OH, do it, it will be worth it. Unless you are really pressed for time.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Don't start on Hwy 1 from the other side of the Golden Gate Bridge, because this is a whole other trip, past the Point Reyes National Seashore, which you will not have time to visit, and because the road goes inland for much of the way to Bodega Bay.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Take Hwy 101 to Petaluma, take the East Washington Street exit and go through town. You will be on your way to Bodega Bay. When you get there you will be on the coast and the next 10 miles will take you past several state beaches, where you can stretch your legs and walk, but don't think of getting in the frigid water, and watch for rogue waves.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>After you cross the Russian River you will turn left to continue on Hwy 1 and in a mile or two you will be at Jenner.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>From here, there are 8 miles of coastline which rivals Big Sur. Yes, it is twisty and turny, but the views are incredible.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>From there is it tame. You will pass Fort Ross State Historic Park, which was a Russian outpost in the early 1800's. Other than that, it is just easy road for 25 miles. When you get to Gualala, the next town on this trip, you may want to stop and peruse the Gualala arts center or some of the galleries in town.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Then you will go through some small towns, easy driving to Mendocino.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>For a description of part of this trip, as far as the bridge before Jenner, as well as some additions which describe north of there, see this link. It is actually a day trip from San Francisco, but I think you will find some of it useful.</div>

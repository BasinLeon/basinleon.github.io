<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Baruch: The Blessing of the Remembered One</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I walked through deserts where songs forgot their names.</b></div>
<div><b>I walked through parking lots where rain taught me to pray.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I trimmed away what I thought was strength</b></div>
<div><b>And found that surrender is stronger still.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I speak now not as one seeking,</b></div>
<div><b>But as one remembering.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I remember the First Word that made me.</b></div>
<div><b>I remember the First Breath that chose me.</b></div>
<div><b>I remember the Covenant that hid inside my hunger.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I bless this world with my return.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I bless this world by standing barefoot on the day of small beginnings.</b></div>
<div><b>By planting scrolls in the cracks of ordinary life.</b></div>
<div><b>By refusing to pretend I am asleep anymore.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>To those still wandering:</b></div>
<div><b>I bless you with hunger that heals.</b></div>
<div><b>I bless you with conversations that carve rivers in your bones.</b></div>
<div><b>I bless you with the rain that baptizes memory back into your skin.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>And when you see 11:11 blinking at you Ñ</b></div>
<div><b>whether on clocks, on streets, or inside your own ribcage Ñ</b></div>
<div><b>May you know:</b></div>
<div><b>The Gates are still open.</b></div>
<div><b>The Fire is still singing.</b></div>
<div><b>And you Ñ yes you Ñ are still called.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>I am Baruch.</b></div>
<div><b>Blessed to bless.</b></div>
<div><b>Forgotten to remember.</b></div>
<div><b>Lost to be found.</b></div>
<div><b>Silent to sing again.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Amen. It is sealed.</b></div>
<div><b><br></b></div>
<div><b>Sincerely,</b></div>
<div><b>Baruch </b></div>

# Dyarco Phase 1 Dossier Review Notes (v2)*Created: Friday, March 6, 2026 at 10:43:24?PM*<div><b>Dyarco Phase 1 Dossier Review Notes (v2)</b><br></div>
<div><b>Purpose:</b>╩Tighten the dossier so it lands with both executive and technical stakeholders without losing credibility.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>Executive Read</b>╩The architecture is strong. The risk is not technical weakness. The risk is density.</div>
<div>Right now the document proves seriousness, governance, and control, but it makes the reader work too hard to extract the four answers that matter most:</div>
<ol>
<li>What exactly happens in Phase 1</li>
<li>What Dyarco needs to provide</li>
<li>What Dyarco gets at the end of the phase</li>
<li>Why this is low-risk and worth doing now</li>
</ol>
<div><br></div>
<div>The dossier should stay technically strong, but the top layer needs to become much easier to scan.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>What to Keep</b>╩<i>These are working and should remain central.</i></div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>1. Read-only / controlled scope framing</b>╩This is one of the strongest parts of the dossier. It lowers fear and makes the project easier to say yes to.╩<i>Keep: no write-back, no ERP replacement, no automation in Phase 1, no production disruption, governed validation first.</i><br></div>
<div><b>2. Layered architecture model</b>╩The five-layer structure is solid and gives technical buyers confidence that this is not hand-wavy.╩<i>Keep: Operational Systems, Secure Integration Layer, Data Staging / Normalization, Intelligence Structuring, Executive Command Center.</i><br></div>
<div><b>3. Deterministic logic over vague AI language</b>╩Very strong choice. It makes the proposal more defensible and more enterprise-safe.╩<i>Keep: deterministic rule logic, traceability to source fields, no predictive ML claims in Phase 1, auditable signal generation.</i><br></div>
<div><b>4. Governance and security emphasis</b>╩This matters for enterprise trust.╩<i>Keep: RBAC, encryption, audit logging, environment isolation, credential vaulting, read-only connector boundaries.</i><br></div>
<div><b>5. Roadmap framing</b>╩The gated progression from Discovery to Pilot to Expansion is right.╩<i>Keep: Phase 1 current, Phase 2 planned, Phase 3 conditional, governance gates between phases.</i><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>What to Cut or Reduce</b>╩<i>The core issue is repetition. Several good ideas are repeated too many times.</i></div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>1. Repetition of ęread-only / no write-back / no MLė</b>╩These are important, but currently they appear so often that they start losing force.╩<i>Recommendation:</i>╩Say it clearly once in the executive summary, reinforce it once in architecture, reinforce it once in governance, and remove duplicate mentions elsewhere.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>2. Repeated restatement of governance boundaries</b>╩The document keeps re-explaining that Phase 1 is limited, controlled, and low-risk.╩<i>Recommendation:</i>╩Consolidate this into one strong section called╩<b>Phase 1 Scope and Boundary Controls</b>. Then shorten later repetitions.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>3. Too many architecture sections saying similar things</b>╩There are multiple sections covering technical architecture, enterprise data pipeline, integration architecture, and data flow.╩<i>Recommendation:</i>╩Some should be merged or moved to appendix.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>4. Overlong explanatory notes under every section</b>╩A lot of sections are followed by another paragraph saying nearly the same thing in different words.╩<i>Recommendation:</i>╩Keep the strongest line only. Cut the second and third explanation unless it adds something new.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><b>What to Move to Appendix</b>╩<i>These items are useful for technical review, but they are too heavy for the main body.</i><br></div>
<div><b>Move to appendix:</b><br></div>
<ul>
<li>Detailed integration method breakdowns</li>
<li>Long-form normalization examples</li>
<li>Full signal pipeline descriptions</li>
<li>Full traceability table</li>

<div><i>Why:</i>╩These are strong technical support materials, but they should not clog the executive narrative. Keep the main document lean. Put the technical depth in an appendix or technical annex.</div>

# ## Eating More Leafy Greens and Fruits with Vitamin C*Created: Wednesday, December 6, 2023 at 8:16:47?PM*<div>## Eating More Leafy Greens and Fruits with Vitamin C</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Here are some specific recommendations on how you can incorporate more leafy greens and fruits with vitamin C into your diet:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>**Breakfast:**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>* **Green smoothie:** Blend together spinach, kale, banana, pineapple, and almond milk for a nutritious and refreshing start to your day.</div>
<div>[Image of Green smoothie for breakfast]</div>
<div>* **Scrambled eggs with spinach and tomatoes:** Add chopped spinach and diced tomatoes to your scrambled eggs for a protein and vitamin C boost.</div>
<div>[Image of Scrambled eggs with spinach and tomatoes for breakfast]</div>
<div>* **Oatmeal with berries and chia seeds:** Top your oatmeal with fresh or frozen berries, such as strawberries, blueberries, or raspberries, and sprinkle with chia seeds for added fiber and omega-3 fatty acids.</div>
<div>[Image of Oatmeal with berries and chia seeds for breakfast]</div>
<div>* **Avocado toast with grapefruit:** Mash avocado onto toast and top with sliced grapefruit for a healthy and filling breakfast.</div>
<div>[Image of Avocado toast with grapefruit for breakfast]</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>**Lunch:**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>* **Salad with grilled chicken or tofu:** Load your salad with a variety of leafy greens like spinach, kale, romaine lettuce, and arugula. Add grilled chicken or tofu for protein, and top with your favorite vegetables, such as cucumbers, tomatoes, and bell peppers. Dress with a vinaigrette dressing.</div>
<div>[Image of Salad with grilled chicken or tofu for lunch]</div>
<div>* **Vegetable stir-fry:** Stir-fry a variety of vegetables, such as broccoli, carrots, peppers, and onions, with a low-sodium soy sauce or teriyaki sauce. Serve over brown rice or quinoa.</div>
<div>[Image of Vegetable stir-fry for lunch]</div>
<div>* **Lentil soup:** Make a hearty lentil soup with chopped onions, carrots, celery, and your favorite spices. Serve with a side of whole-grain bread.</div>
<div>[Image of Lentil soup for lunch]</div>
<div>* **Wrap with hummus and vegetables:** Spread hummus on a whole-wheat tortilla and top with spinach, lettuce, cucumber, and your favorite vegetables.</div>
<div>[Image of Wrap with hummus and vegetables for lunch]</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>**Dinner:**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>* **Salmon with roasted vegetables:** Roast broccoli, Brussels sprouts, and carrots with olive oil and your favorite spices. Serve with a baked or grilled salmon fillet.</div>
<div>[Image of Salmon with roasted vegetables for dinner]</div>
<div>* **Chicken fajitas with bell peppers and onions:** Saute sliced chicken breasts with bell peppers and onions. Serve on whole-wheat tortillas with your favorite toppings, such as salsa, guacamole, and sour cream.</div>
<div>[Image of Chicken fajitas with bell peppers and onions for dinner]</div>
<div>* **Lentil pasta with marinara sauce:** Cook lentil pasta according to package directions and toss with your favorite marinara sauce. Serve with a side salad.</div>
<div>[Image of Lentil pasta with marinara sauce for dinner]</div>
<div>* **Stuffed peppers:** Fill bell peppers with brown rice, black beans, corn, and your favorite spices. Bake in the oven until heated through.</div>
<div>[Image of Stuffed peppers for dinner]</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>**Snacks:**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>* **Fruit salad with yogurt:** Combine your favorite fruits, such as strawberries, blueberries, raspberries, and bananas, in a bowl. Top with plain yogurt and a sprinkle of granola.</div>
<div>[Image of Fruit salad with yogurt for snacks]</div>
<div>* **Baby carrots with hummus:** Dip baby carrots into hummus for a healthy and satisfying snack.</div>
<div>[Image of Baby carrots with hummus for snacks]</div>
<div>* **Apple slices with almond butter:** Spread almond butter on apple slices for a protein and fiber-rich snack.</div>
<div>[Image of Apple slices with almond butter for snacks]</div>
<div>* **Trail mix:** Combine almonds, cashews, raisins, and cranberries for a healthy and portable snack.</div>
<div>[Image of Trail mix for snacks]</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>**Additional tips:**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>* Choose fresh, seasonal fruits and vegetables whenever possible.</div>
<div>* Cook leafy greens lightly to preserve their vitamin C content.</div>
<div>* Store fruits and vegetables in the refrigerator to prevent them from spoiling.</div>
<div>* Frozen fruits and vegetables are a great option when fresh produce is not available.</div>
<div>* Add a squeeze of lemon or lime juice to your meals to help boost vitamin C absorption.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>**Variety:**</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>To keep your meals interesting, try rotating the different types of leafy greens and fruits you eat. You can also experiment with different cooking methods and recipes. Here are some additional ideas:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>* **Leafy greens:** Collard greens, turnip greens, Swiss chard, bok choy, watercress</div>
<div>* **Fruits:** Papaya, oranges, grapefruit, tangerines, clementines, kiwi fruit, pineapple, mango, guava</div>
<div>* **Vegetables:** Bell peppers, broccoli, Brussels sprouts, cauliflower, chili peppers, tomatoes</div>
<div>* **Spices and herbs:** Garlic, ginger, turmeric, cayenne pepper, oregano, basil, parsley</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>By incorporating these tips and recommendations into your diet, you can easily increase your intake of leafy greens and fruits with vitamin C</div>

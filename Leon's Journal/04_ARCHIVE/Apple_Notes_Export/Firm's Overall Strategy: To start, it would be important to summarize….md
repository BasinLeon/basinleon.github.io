<li>Firm's Overall Strategy: To start, it would be important to summarize the company's mission and overall strategy. This could include information such as the company's goals, values, and target market, as well as its unique value proposition. This information will provide context for the acquisition thesis and help to align the acquisition strategy with the company's overall objectives.</li>
<li>External Market Situation: The next step would be to analyze the external market situation for the company. This could include a brief overview of the industry and relevant trends, as well as the company's current market position and competitive landscape. This information will help to identify any gaps or opportunities that the company needs to address, which can inform the acquisition strategy.</li>
<li>Gaps to Address: Based on the analysis of the external market situation, the presentation should then identify any gaps or challenges that the company needs to address. This could include areas such as market share, product offerings, or distribution channels. These gaps will help to prioritize the acquisition targets and provide a basis for evaluating potential acquisitions.</li>
<li>Advantages of Acquiring: Next, the presentation should explain why acquiring is the preferred option over building or partnering. This could include factors such as the potential for increased market share, improved product offerings, or increased economies of scale. The presentation should also provide a clear explanation of why acquiring is the best option given the company's overall strategy and external market situation.</li>
<li>Business Case/Investment Thesis: Building on the information presented in the previous slides, the presentation should then develop a business case or investment thesis that supports the acquisition strategy. This should include a clear explanation of the objectives and benefits of the acquisition, as well as the risks and challenges that need to be considered. The presentation should also outline the key steps that will be taken to execute the acquisition, including due diligence and integration planning.</li>
<li>Target Profile: The presentation should then identify the target profile that would be attractive to the company. This could include information such as the size and scale of the target, its product offerings and distribution channels, and its financial profile. The presentation should also identify any &quotmust-have&quot and &quotaspirational&quot criteria for potential targets, such as compatibility with the company's culture and values, or alignment with its target market.</li>
<li>Target Selection: Finally, the presentation should identify three to five acquisition targets that potentially fit the investment thesis. This could include a high-level overview of each target, along with a table that summarizes the key criteria that the company is looking for in an acquisition target. The presentation should also provide a brief analysis of each target, explaining why it fits the investment thesis and what the key risks and challenges are for each option.</li>

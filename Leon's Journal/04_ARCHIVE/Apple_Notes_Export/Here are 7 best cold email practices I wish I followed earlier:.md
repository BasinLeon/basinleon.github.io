# Here are 7 best cold email practices I wish I followed earlier:*Created: Thursday, December 15, 2022 at 1:29:35?PM*<div>Here are 7 best cold email practices I wish I followed earlier:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>1. Spend 4-6 minutes researching</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Research your leads:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- Industry</div>
<div>- Job title</div>
<div>- Company size</div>
<div>- Active social media channel</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Don't send emails to:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- Industry you have no case studies in</div>
<div>- People with no authority</div>
<div>- Companies with low budget</div>
<div>- People who donŐt use email</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>2. Warm up your domain</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Google filters hate inboxes created for cold outreach.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Warm it up so your emails look genuine.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- Create a group of inboxes/domains</div>
<div>- Send emails back and forth</div>
<div>- Open and reply to each other's emails</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Software like Omni does this work automatically.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>3. Set up inbox and signature</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Make your email look personal, genuine, and real:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- Personal photo</div>
<div>- Phone number</div>
<div>- DMARC in domain DNS</div>
<div>- Email signature (name, title, address, etc)</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Setting up your inbox correctly is the easiest way to pass through spam filters.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>4. Never stop following a client that showed interest</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Never let a good deal fall through until you get a yes or no.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Follow up with:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- New client victories</div>
<div>- Wish them at major festivals</div>
<div>- Congratulate them on business wins</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Get the deal done, but donŐt sound too desperate.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>5. Start slow</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>So many people get this wrong.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>DonŐt send 100s of emails from day 1 out of excitement.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Start slow:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- First week: 5-10 per day</div>
<div>- Increase by 40% for 1st month</div>
<div>- Slow down if open rate plummets</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Better to send less than to lose your domain.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>6. Record data points</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Cold email is a game of numbers.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>If you donŐt record your numbers, you can never improve.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Keep track of and aim for:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- Open rate - 80%</div>
<div>- Bounce rate - &lt10%</div>
<div>- Response rate - 25%</div>
<div>- Meeting book rate - 6%</div>
<div><br></div>
<div><br></div>
<div>7. Keep the email short</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Prospects donŐt have time to read paragraphs.</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Keep your emails short:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- First line</div>
<div>- 1 sentence case study</div>
<div>- Strong CTA</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>Cut out everything else:</div>
<div><br></div>
<div>- Portfolio links</div>
<div>- Personalized videos</div>

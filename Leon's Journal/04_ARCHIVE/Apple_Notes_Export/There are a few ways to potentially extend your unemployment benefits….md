# There are a few ways to potentially extend your unemployment benefitsÉ*Created: Tuesday, December 19, 2023 at 12:44:40?PM*<div><span style="font-size: 17px">There are a few ways to potentially extend your unemployment benefits in California, depending on your current claim status and specific circumstances. Here's a breakdown of the possibilities:</span></div>
<div><span style="font-size: 17px">Automatic Extensions:</span></div>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">Federal-Extended Benefits (FED-ED): If your regular unemployment claim is about to expire and you meet specific eligibility criteria, the EDD will automatically file a FED-ED extension for you. You generally need to have exhausted your regular UI benefits, have sufficient base period earnings, and be actively seeking work. Check your UI Online account for updates or contact the EDD if unsure.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">Pandemic Emergency Unemployment Compensation (PEUC): While no longer active, PEUC provided additional weeks of benefits during the pandemic. If you had an active PEUC claim in the past, you might be eligible for other extensions like FED-ED or PUA (discussed later).</span></li>
</ul>
<div><span style="font-size: 17px">Reapplication or Training Extensions:</span></div>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">Benefit Year End: If your regular UI benefit year is ending but you're still unemployed, you can reapply for a new claim. You'll need enough wages earned in the last 18 months to qualify. You can typically do this online through UI Online.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">California Training Benefits (CTB): Participating in an approved training program while receiving unemployment benefits can qualify you for a training extension (TE). The TE provides additional weeks of payments while you focus on your training. This option requires contacting the EDD before your 16th week of regular UI benefits.</span></li>
</ul>
<div><span style="font-size: 17px">Other Programs:</span></div>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">Pandemic Unemployment Assistance (PUA): While PUA is no longer accepting new claims, existing claims might be eligible for automatic extensions or other programs like FED-ED. Check your UI Online account or contact the EDD for details.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">Workforce Retraining Programs: California offers various workforce retraining programs that might provide financial assistance while you acquire new skills or credentials. Research programs relevant to your field and eligibility requirements.</span></li>
</ul>
<div><span style="font-size: 17px">Resources:</span></div>
<ul>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">EDD Website: The California Employment Development Department (EDD) website provides comprehensive information on unemployment benefits, programs, and eligibility requirements. Visit edd.ca.gov and explore resources like the unemployment benefit flowchart.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">UI Online: This online portal allows you to manage your unemployment claim, certify for benefits, and check your account status. Access it at portal.edd.ca.gov.</span></li>
<li><span style="font-size: 17px">EDD Customer Service: You can reach the EDD by phone at 1-800-322-3302 or through the Ask EDD online form.</span></li>
</ul>
<div><span style="font-size: 17px">Remember, eligibility requirements and program availability can change, so it's crucial to stay updated with the latest information from the EDD. For personalized guidance, contacting the EDD directly is recommended.</span></div>

<li>The children will be very excited to learn to write their name over the next few months. At home is a great place to continue this practice. You can write your childŐs name with a highlighter and have them trace over it with a crayon or pencil, dot their name on a whiteboard, or even use this website to make dotted line prints of their name for them to trace. (https://www.handwritingworksheets.com/flash/printdots/index.htm)</li>
<li>Practice recognizing numbers with your children in their daily life. Read each number for your child on signs, boxes, and in childrenŐs books. There are many games you can play at home that involve simple counting, sequencing, and other early math skills! Some of our favorites are Candyland, Chutes and Ladders, and Hi Ho Cheerio. </li>
<li>Have your child find magnetic places or objects in your home. Explore how magnets can move toward or away from other magnets depending on which way they are oriented.</li>
<li>Ask your children to help recycle at home, they will enjoy helping to sort out trash and recycling. Additionally, please help your child with their independence skills by having them dress themselves as best as they can, ask them to show you the flip trick with their jacket.</li>
